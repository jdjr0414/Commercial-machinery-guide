Quick Answer

Startups (under 2 years in business) can finance commercial equipment, but face higher hurdles than established businesses. The most successful startup financing applications include: 680+ personal credit score, 20–30% down payment, prior industry experience (even as an employee), used equipment for lower loan exposure, equipment with strong collateral value (Cat, Komatsu, John Deere), and a personal guarantee. Approval Factors

What Helps and Hurts Startup Equipment Financing Approval

Lenders evaluate startup equipment applications differently than established business applications because there is no business track record to review. Instead, they focus on the owner's personal credit, the quality of the equipment as collateral, available down payment, and signals that the business owner has the skills to succeed. Building Your Application

How to Build the Strongest Possible Startup Application

Startup equipment financing approvals are not random — lenders are looking for specific signals that reduce their risk. Addressing each of these areas before you apply materially improves your odds:

  • Check your personal credit — Know your score before any lender does. Dispute errors at least 60 days before applying.
  • Choose brand-name equipment — Cat, Komatsu, John Deere, Kubota, and Bobcat are the most financeable brands for startups. Avoid unknown or Chinese-branded machinery as your first financed purchase.
  • Accumulate 0-20% down (0% available for qualified borrowers) payment — This single factor may be the most important one. It reduces lender risk and signals serious commitment.
  • Document your industry experience — Former employer references, certifications, operator licenses, or any documented history in the field are valuable. Include a brief letter explaining your background.
  • Get a first customer letter if possible — A signed contract, letter of intent, or even a written customer commitment for your first project dramatically strengthens the application narrative.
  • Apply through the OEM dealer first — Before approaching banks or alt lenders, walk into the dealer for your chosen brand and ask about their financing program.
  • Offer a personal guarantee proactively — Do not wait to be asked. Volunteering a personal guarantee signals confidence and seriousness.

Common Questions

Equipment Financing for Startups — FAQ

Can a brand-new business get equipment financing?
Yes, brand-new businesses can obtain equipment financing, but the requirements are significantly more stringent than for established businesses. The factors that most help a startup get approved are: a personal credit score of 680 or higher, a down payment of 20-30%, financing brand-name equipment (Caterpillar, Komatsu, John Deere, Bobcat) with strong resale value, documented prior industry experience by the business owner, and a willingness to sign a personal guarantee. OEM vendor financing programs (equipment lenders, equipment lenders, equipment lenders) are often the most startup-friendly option, followed by alternative equipment lenders. Traditional banks are the least likely to approve a business with under 12 months of operating history.
What credit score do I need as a startup to finance equipment?
For startups (under 2 years in business), most lenders require a higher personal credit score than for established businesses — typically 680+ for OEM programs and alternative lenders, and 700+ for bank programs. The reasoning is simple: with no business track record to evaluate, the lender relies almost entirely on the owner's personal creditworthiness as a proxy for repayment reliability. A score below 640 as a startup will face very limited options. Above 700, with strong down payment and industry experience, many OEM and alternative lender programs become accessible. Some specialized startup equipment lenders work with scores as low as 620, but typically require 25-30% down and charge higher interest rates. See our equipment financing credit requirements guide for a full breakdown by credit tier.
Is an SBA loan better than OEM financing for a startup?
It depends on your timeline and equipment type. SBA 7(a) and 504 loans offer competitive rates (often 7-10%) and lower down payments (10-20%) and are startup-friendly, but they take 30-90 days to close — which is too slow if you need equipment for a project already underway. OEM financing programs (equipment lenders, equipment lenders, equipment lenders) typically close in 1-5 days and are often equally startup-friendly when you are buying that brand's equipment. For most startups buying brand-name equipment with an OEM financing program, OEM financing is the faster and simpler path. SBA loans become more advantageous for large purchases ($500K+), equipment brands without captive financing, or when you need the lowest possible rate and have time to wait for the processing window.
How much down payment does a startup need for equipment financing?
Most startup equipment financing programs require 20-30% down payment, compared to 10-15% for established businesses with strong credit. The down payment serves two purposes: it reduces the lender's exposure (financing a smaller percentage of the equipment's value), and it demonstrates that the business owner has genuine capital commitment. For SBA 504 loans, the business owner contributes only 10%, the SBA provides 40% via a certified development company, and a lender covers the remaining 50% — making SBA 504 one of the lowest-down-payment options available to startups. For OEM programs on high-value brands, startup down payments can sometimes be as low as 15% with 680+ credit. Having 3-6 months of projected payments in cash reserves beyond the down payment also improves approval odds.
What types of equipment are easiest for startups to finance?
The easiest equipment for startups to finance has two key characteristics: strong brand recognition with proven resale values, and broad market demand that ensures quick liquidation if needed. Mini excavators (Cat, Kubota, Bobcat, John Deere), compact tractors (John Deere, Kubota, Massey Ferguson), small forklifts (Toyota, Crown), and standard construction equipment from major manufacturers are consistently the most accessible startup financing categories. Medical and dental equipment is also startup-friendly because healthcare providers have steady, documented revenue potential. The hardest equipment for startups to finance is niche or specialty machinery, Chinese-branded construction equipment, heavily customized equipment, or old used equipment — all of which have uncertain resale values that make lenders uncomfortable.
Should a startup buy new or used equipment?
For most startups, quality used equipment from a reputable brand represents the optimal balance of affordability and financability. A used Cat mini excavator at $35,000 vs. a new one at $75,000 cuts the monthly payment roughly in half — a critical advantage when first-year cash flow is unpredictable. Used equipment also means lower depreciation risk since a 3-year-old Cat has already absorbed most of its early depreciation curve. Stick to well-maintained, major-brand used equipment within 3-7 years old, purchased from a reputable dealer or auction house. New equipment makes more sense when OEM promotional financing (0% APR periods, deferred payments) is available and offsets the higher price, or when the equipment will be duty-cycled heavily enough that reliability is mission-critical from day one. See our new vs. used equipment guide for detailed analysis.
How does having prior industry experience help a startup get financing?
Prior industry experience is one of the most underrated factors in startup equipment financing approval. Lenders evaluate whether the business owner has the skills and knowledge to actually operate the equipment profitably and sustain loan payments. An owner who spent 8 years operating excavators for another company before starting their own excavating business is a fundamentally different risk profile from someone entering construction with no prior experience. Many lenders — especially OEM programs and SBA-approved lenders — explicitly consider industry experience in their startup underwriting. Documenting prior experience through former employer references, operator certifications, contractor licenses, or even published project portfolios strengthens the application. If you have 5+ years of relevant experience, it can partially substitute for the business track record you haven't yet built.
What is the fastest way for a startup to get equipment financing approved?
The fastest path to startup equipment financing approval is through an OEM dealer financing program for brand-name equipment. Walking into a Cat, John Deere, Kubota, or Bobcat dealer and applying through their captive financing arm typically yields same-day or next-day decisions for qualified applicants. These programs are designed to move equipment off the lot, so they are generally more flexible with startups than banks while still offering competitive rates. Alternative equipment lenders like Balboa Capital and TimePayment also make same-day decisions on applications up to $250,000. For large purchases requiring SBA financing, plan for 30-90 days. Preparation dramatically speeds the process — check your credit score beforehand, organize your business documents (entity formation, EIN letter, bank statements), have your down payment confirmed and ready, and bring proof of industry experience to your first meeting.
