SBA 504 Financing for Gas Stations

The SBA 504 loan program is the most popular financing vehicle for complete gas station buildouts because it covers both real estate and equipment at favorable terms:

  • Structure: 50% conventional bank loan + 40% SBA/CDC loan + 10% borrower equity
  • Real estate term: 20 years (reduces monthly payment significantly)
  • Equipment term: 10 years (USTs, dispensers, canopy)
  • Down payment: 10% minimum for existing businesses; 15–20% for startups
  • Environmental requirement: SBA 504 lenders require Phase I and often Phase II environmental assessments before funding
  • Best use case: Owner-occupied gas station with real estate — if you own the land and building, SBA 504 is optimal

For buyers leasing rather than owning the site, equipment-only financing (for dispensers, tanks, canopy) can be structured separately through petroleum equipment lenders at 36–84 month terms with environmental insurance required.

Frequently Asked Questions — Gas Station Equipment Financing

How much does it cost to open a gas station?

A complete gas station buildout costs $660,000–$3,535,000+. Land and building is the largest variable ($200,000–$2,000,000+). Equipment alone (USTs + dispensers + canopy + POS) typically costs $250,000–$650,000. Working capital and initial fuel inventory add $100,000–$250,000. SBA 504 loans cover both real estate and equipment with as little as 10% down.

How much does a Gilbarco fuel dispenser cost?

Gilbarco Veeder-Root (Greensboro, NC — Fortive Corporation) dispensers range from $3,500–$5,500 for the entry-level Encore 700 S to $9,500–$16,500 for the Insite 360 connected dispenser. The most common Encore 700 multi-product model costs $4,500–$7,500. EMV-compliant models add $1,000–$2,000. Used dispensers sell for $1,200–$4,000.

What are the EPA requirements for underground storage tanks?

EPA 40 CFR Part 280 requires: double-wall tanks for all new installations, spill containment and overfill protection, corrosion protection, leak detection (monthly monitoring or automatic equipment), financial assurance for cleanup costs, and EPA Class A, B, and C operator training. State programs are typically stricter than federal. A complete 4-tank installation (3 gasoline + 1 diesel) costs $80,000–$200,000 in tanks and installation alone.

What is the best way to finance a gas station?

The SBA 504 loan is the most popular vehicle: 50% conventional bank + 40% SBA/CDC + 10% borrower equity, with 20-year real estate terms and 10-year equipment terms. Environmental insurance ($50,000–$150,000/year) is required by most lenders as a condition of UST financing. For leased sites, equipment-only financing can be arranged separately at 36–84 month terms.

What licenses are required to operate a gas station?

Required licenses include: EPA UST Class A, B, C operator training; state petroleum storage tank permit; state fuel retailer license; fire department inspection and permit; weights and measures certification; business license; sales tax permit for fuel; local zoning approval; food service permit if selling prepared food; and alcohol license if selling beer/wine.

What is the difference between independent and branded dealer?

An independent buys fuel from any supplier with maximum price flexibility but no brand support. A branded dealer (Shell, BP, Chevron, ExxonMobil) gets brand recognition and marketing but must purchase fuel from the brand at contract prices. DODO (Dealer Owned, Dealer Operated) means you own the site and choose your own supplier. First-time operators often choose branded dealer arrangements for the support structure and easier financing relationships.

How much does a gas station make per month?

Fuel margin is $0.15–$0.35 per gallon. An average independent station selling 80,000–150,000 gallons/month earns $12,000–$52,500/month in fuel margin. C-store sales add $30,000–$150,000/month at 25–35% margins. Total net income for owner-operated independent stations typically runs $3,000–$25,000/month, varying heavily by location, traffic, competition, and whether a car wash or prepared food program is included.

Why does petroleum equipment financing require environmental insurance?

USTs represent significant environmental liability — a leaking tank can trigger remediation costs of $100,000–$5,000,000+. Lenders require environmental insurance ($50,000–$150,000/year) to protect their collateral value. Most state UST programs also require financial assurance either through insurance or state cleanup funds. This environmental insurance requirement makes gas station financing more specialized than standard equipment loans.
