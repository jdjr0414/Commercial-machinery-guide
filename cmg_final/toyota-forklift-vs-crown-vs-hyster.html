IC Propane vs Electric — Which Type Should You Finance?

Before comparing brands, the IC vs electric decision has the biggest financial impact:

  • IC Propane Forklifts (Toyota 8FGCU25, Hyster H50FT): Lower purchase price ($22K–$31K), work indoors and outdoors, faster refueling (swap propane tank in 5 minutes). Higher operating cost (propane fuel) and maintenance vs electric. Better for facilities that use equipment both inside and outside.
  • Electric Forklifts (Crown FC 5200, Toyota 8FBE25): Higher purchase price ($28K–$58K) plus battery ($6,000–$15,000 for replacement). Zero emissions for indoor air quality. Lower fuel cost (electricity vs propane). Best for indoor warehouse use with available charging infrastructure. Crown's 10-year motor warranty dramatically reduces long-term maintenance cost for electric fleet operators.

Crown's 10-Year Motor Warranty — The Electric Fleet Cost Advantage

Crown Equipment's AC2 dual-AC-motor system in the FC 5200 and SC 6000 series carries a 10-year warranty on the drive motors — dramatically longer than Toyota's 2-year and Hyster's 2-year warranties on comparable electric forklifts. For fleet operators, this warranty difference has significant financial implications:

  • Electric motor replacement cost: $4,000–$9,000 per motor
  • A 10-unit Toyota electric fleet might face 3–5 motor replacements in years 3–7 at $5,000–$7,000 each = $15,000–$35,000 in unexpected costs
  • The same 10-unit Crown fleet has those costs covered by warranty for 10 years
  • Crown's higher initial price ($32K–$58K vs Toyota's $28K–$40K) can be offset by warranty savings on fleets of 5+ units

Frequently Asked Questions — Toyota vs Crown vs Hyster Financing

Which forklift brand holds its value best?

Toyota consistently holds the best resale value in the US — 45–55% of new at 5 years for IC forklifts. Crown holds 40–50% and Hyster 35–45% at 5 years. Toyota's resale premium is driven by brand recognition, the largest US parts network, and a well-documented reliability reputation. The spread narrows at 8–10 years when all three brands are trading at 20–35% of original value.

Which is better for warehouse use — Crown FC 5200 or Toyota electric?

For dedicated indoor warehouse use, Crown FC 5200's AC2 dual-motor technology, ergonomic operator compartment, and 10-year motor warranty make it the preferred choice for high-volume operations with long shifts. Toyota electric forklifts are equally capable and have better resale. For fleets of 5+ electric units with long-term service agreements, Crown's warranty advantage and fleet service programs typically win on total cost.

How much does a Toyota 8FGCU25 cost vs Crown and Hyster?

The Toyota 8FGCU25 (5,000 lb IC LPG) costs $22,000–$30,000. The Crown C-5 equivalent costs $22,000–$42,000. The Hyster H50FT costs $22,000–$31,000. In the electric segment, Toyota 8FBE25 costs $28,000–$40,000 and Crown FC 5200 costs $32,000–$58,000 — Crown's electric premium reflects the AC2 motor technology and 10-year warranty.

Does Toyota offer forklift financing?

Yes. Toyota Financial Services (Plano, Texas) provides OEM forklift financing through Toyota Material Handling dealers with 24–60 month terms. Buyers should also compare third-party commercial equipment lenders, who often offer lower rates than OEM programs on standard credit profiles.

Which forklift brand has the best parts availability?

Toyota has the broadest US parts network with the largest installed base of any forklift brand globally. Crown parts are excellent for fleet service customers with Crown service agreements. Hyster-Yale's network is good in most US markets but has gaps in rural areas. Overall: Toyota wins on nationwide parts availability; Crown wins for fleet service agreement customers.

Is Crown forklift made in the USA?

Yes. Crown Equipment Corporation (New Bremen, Ohio) is privately owned by the Dicke family since 1945 and manufactures all forklifts in New Bremen, Ohio; Kinston, North Carolina; and Greencastle, Indiana. Crown is one of the last major forklift manufacturers maintaining complete US production. Toyota forklifts are manufactured in Columbus, Indiana, but Toyota Material Handling has a Japanese parent company (Toyota Industries). Hyster-Yale manufactures in Greenville, NC; Sulligent, AL; and Berea, KY — all US plants.
