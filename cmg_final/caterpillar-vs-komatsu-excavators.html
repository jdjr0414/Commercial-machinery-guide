Quick Answer

Caterpillar and Komatsu are the two most financeable excavator brands in the world. Cat 320 costs $270,000–$310,000; Komatsu PC210LCi costs $245,000–$285,000 (8–10% less). Komatsu's factory-integrated iMC 2.0 full 3D grade control is included as standard — the equivalent Cat 3D Grade option adds $15,000–$25,000. Cat's US dealer network (~4,000 locations) vastly exceeds Komatsu's (~1,800 locations). Both brands retain 68–72% of value at 3 years — effectively identical in lenders' eyes. For financing purposes, both brands receive identical treatment from third-party lenders.

Common Questions

Cat vs Komatsu Excavators — FAQ

What is the price difference between Cat 320 and Komatsu PC210LCi?
A new Caterpillar 320 costs approximately $270,000–$310,000. A new Komatsu PC210LCi costs approximately $245,000–$285,000 — typically 8–10% less. However, the PC210LCi includes Komatsu's iMC 2.0 full 3D machine control as standard equipment, technology that costs $15,000–$25,000 as an add-on to the Cat 320. When comparing fully-equipped machines with equivalent grade control technology, the price gap narrows significantly or disappears entirely. For financing both machines, see our excavator financing guide.
Which brand has better resale value — Cat or Komatsu?
At 3 years, Cat and Komatsu excavators retain nearly identical residual values: 68–72% of original purchase price for both brands in good condition with normal hours. At 5 years, Cat holds a slight edge: 55–60% vs. 53–58% for Komatsu. At 10 years, Cat tends to retain a more meaningful premium in North American markets. Both brands are considered Tier 1 collateral by equipment lenders — financing terms for Cat and Komatsu are essentially identical from third-party lenders. See our new vs used excavator guide for how residual values affect the buy decision.
Is Komatsu iMC better than Cat Grade control?
Komatsu's iMC 2.0 (Intelligent Machine Control) is a factory-integrated full 3D machine control system standard on the PC210LCi. Cat Grade 2D is standard on the Cat 320; full 3D Cat Grade requires a $15,000–$25,000 factory or aftermarket option. Both systems deliver precision grade control to approximately ±0.04 ft. Komatsu's advantage is factory integration — designed as a complete system. Cat's advantage is broader aftermarket support and the ability to choose from alternative grade control providers (Trimble, Leica, Topcon). For contractors who need full 3D grade control, the Komatsu's standard inclusion represents meaningful value.
How does Cat Financial compare to Komatsu Financial?
Cat Financial and Komatsu Financial are both captive OEM lenders offering competitive financing exclusively for their brand's equipment. Both provide: streamlined dealer-based approvals; periodic promotional rates on new equipment; terms from 36–84 months; and strong residual value knowledge supporting favorable loan-to-value ratios. Cat Financial has a slight edge in promotional program frequency due to higher North American dealer volume. Neither lender finances the competing brand's equipment. For current promotional rates, contact your local dealer. See our Cat financing guide and Komatsu financing guide for specifics.
Which is better for a startup contractor — Cat or Komatsu?
For a startup contractor's first excavator, both brands are excellent choices with equivalent financing accessibility. Komatsu's lower purchase price and standard iMC grade control benefit new contractors who need grade control capability and want to minimize initial capital outlay. Cat's larger dealer network (4,000 vs. 1,800 US locations) means service and parts are more consistently available — meaningful for contractors who may not be near a major dealer. Either brand on a 5–6 year loan represents a sound starting choice. Consider which brand's dealer is closer to your primary work area. For startup financing strategy, see our construction equipment financing guide.
How does parts availability compare between Cat and Komatsu?
Caterpillar's US network at ~4,000 locations significantly exceeds Komatsu's ~1,800 US locations. Cat parts are generally available within 24–48 hours. Komatsu parts are equally available at their dealers but dealer density is lower in rural areas. In major metro areas, both brands have strong coverage. In rural or remote areas, Cat's network density may reduce downtime when urgent parts are needed. For contractors working in metro markets, the difference is negligible. See our Cat 320 vs PC210 financing comparison for more detailed ownership cost analysis.
Should I buy a used Cat or used Komatsu excavator?
For used excavator purchases, focus on condition, service history, hours, and price — not brand preference between Cat and Komatsu. Both hold value nearly identically. Key evaluation points: request complete maintenance records; inspect undercarriage (replacement cost $15,000–$40,000); check hydraulic cylinder condition; review VisionLink or KOMTRAX telematics history for recent models; get a pre-purchase inspection from an independent mechanic. A well-maintained Komatsu at the right price beats a poorly maintained Cat at the same price. For comprehensive used excavator guidance, see our new vs used excavator guide.
Do Cat and Komatsu attachments interchange?
Cat and Komatsu excavator hydraulic attachments do not directly interchange. Each brand uses different quick coupler standards, hydraulic flow rates, and pin configurations. However, most aftermarket attachment manufacturers (Werk-Brau, Nye, Genesis, LaBounty) offer versions configured for both brands' pin specifications. Universal quick couplers from manufacturers like Engcon allow using a broader range of attachments across brands. If you have an existing attachment library built for one brand, that is a practical reason to stay with that brand — coupler conversion costs across a large attachment fleet can be significant.
