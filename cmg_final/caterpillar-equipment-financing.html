Frequently Asked Questions

What does a Cat 320 excavator cost?

A new Cat 320 excavator (2021+ model, 20-ton, 163 HP) costs $258,000–$320,000. A used Cat 320 (5–7 years old) sells for $114,000–$167,000. The Cat 320 is the best-selling excavator model worldwide, meaning there is a massive and liquid secondary market — this translates directly to better financing terms because lenders have high confidence in collateral recovery. equipment lenders offers 0% for 48–60 months on qualifying new 320 purchases when promotions are available.

How does equipment lenders work?

equipment lenders Services Corp. is Caterpillar's captive finance company, headquartered in Nashville, Tennessee. It provides equipment loans, leases, and rental-purchase agreements through authorized Cat dealers. equipment lenders regularly offers promotional rates including 0% for 48–60 months on select new equipment — these promotions are time-limited and model-specific, so ask your dealer what's currently available. Cat Certified Used provides inspected used equipment with extended warranties and better financing rates than non-certified used Cat.

Can you get Cat equipment financing with bad credit?

Caterpillar equipment can be financed with challenged credit through independent specialty lenders, though equipment lenders itself has stricter standards. Independent lenders who regularly finance CAT will consider credit scores as low as 550+ with larger down payments (25–35%) and strong business revenue documentation. CAT's exceptional resale value (68–75% at 3 years) means lenders are more willing to work with challenged credit than they would be for less established brands — the collateral risk is lower.

What is Cat Certified Used equipment?

Cat Certified Used is Caterpillar's pre-owned equipment program. Machines undergo a 140+ point inspection at an authorized Cat dealer, receive necessary repairs and updates, and come with an extended powertrain warranty (12–36 months depending on the program selected). equipment lenders provides preferential financing rates for Certified Used vs non-certified used Cat — typically 0.5–2% lower rates. The combination of warranty coverage and better financing rate often makes Cat Certified Used the best value in the used excavator market.

Where is Caterpillar equipment manufactured?

Caterpillar (Irving, Texas — founded 1925) manufactures equipment at 13+ US plants: East Peoria, Illinois (excavators, motor graders); Decatur, Illinois (large mining equipment, large dozers); Mossville, Illinois (engines and powertrain); Aurora, Illinois (small construction equipment); and Victoria, Texas (wheel loaders). Caterpillar employs approximately 55,000 US workers. Despite being a global manufacturer with facilities in 25+ countries, a significant portion of Cat construction equipment sold in North America is manufactured in the United States.

Why does Caterpillar have the best resale value?

Caterpillar's exceptional resale values (68–75% at 3 years) stem from: universal brand recognition (every contractor and lender worldwide knows the yellow CAT), the densest dealer service network in the industry (parts available everywhere quickly), decades of proven durability data, and self-reinforcing demand (high resale makes contractors willing to pay more new, which justifies high resale, in a virtuous cycle). For buyers, this means CAT equipment has the lowest effective ownership cost when you account for both operating costs and resale recovery.
