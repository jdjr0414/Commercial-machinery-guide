Cat Financial — The OEM Financing Advantage

Cat Financial Services Corp. is a wholly-owned subsidiary of Caterpillar Inc., separate from the equipment manufacturing division. Headquartered in Nashville, Tennessee, Cat Financial manages over $30 billion in managed assets globally — making it one of the largest equipment finance companies in the world.

Cat Financial's 0% promotional offers are among the most valuable in construction equipment — when available, they represent real interest savings of $15,000–$80,000 over the loan term compared to market rates. These promos typically run on new equipment for limited periods (often model year-end or quarterly pushes by dealers).

The Cat Certified Used program is a significant advantage for used equipment buyers. Machines undergo a 140+ point inspection, receive necessary repairs, and come with an extended powertrain warranty (12–36 months). Cat Financial provides better rates on Certified Used than on standard used Cat equipment. For buyers who prefer new-equipment financing confidence with used-equipment pricing, Cat Certified Used is often the best option available in the construction equipment market.

Cat Financial's Smart Commerce platform and PaymentWatch portal allow buyers to shop and get pre-approved online before visiting a dealer. Seasonal and deferred payment structures are available for contractors with seasonal revenue patterns. Independent lenders may win on older used Cat equipment (8+ years) or when business credit doesn't meet Cat Financial's standards — compare both options before committing.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does a Cat 320 excavator cost?

A new Cat 320 excavator (2021+ model, 20-ton, 163 HP) costs $258,000–$320,000. A used Cat 320 (5–7 years old) sells for $114,000–$167,000. The Cat 320 is the best-selling excavator model worldwide, meaning there is a massive and liquid secondary market — this translates directly to better financing terms because lenders have high confidence in collateral recovery. Cat Financial offers 0% for 48–60 months on qualifying new 320 purchases when promotions are available.

How does Cat Financial work?

Cat Financial Services Corp. is Caterpillar's captive finance company, headquartered in Nashville, Tennessee. It provides equipment loans, leases, and rental-purchase agreements through authorized Cat dealers. Cat Financial regularly offers promotional rates including 0% for 48–60 months on select new equipment — these promotions are time-limited and model-specific, so ask your dealer what's currently available. Cat Certified Used provides inspected used equipment with extended warranties and better financing rates than non-certified used Cat.

Can you get Cat equipment financing with bad credit?

Caterpillar equipment can be financed with challenged credit through independent specialty lenders, though Cat Financial itself has stricter standards. Independent lenders who regularly finance CAT will consider credit scores as low as 550–580 with larger down payments (25–35%) and strong business revenue documentation. CAT's exceptional resale value (68–75% at 3 years) means lenders are more willing to work with challenged credit than they would be for less established brands — the collateral risk is lower.

What is Cat Certified Used equipment?

Cat Certified Used is Caterpillar's pre-owned equipment program. Machines undergo a 140+ point inspection at an authorized Cat dealer, receive necessary repairs and updates, and come with an extended powertrain warranty (12–36 months depending on the program selected). Cat Financial provides preferential financing rates for Certified Used vs non-certified used Cat — typically 0.5–2% lower rates. The combination of warranty coverage and better financing rate often makes Cat Certified Used the best value in the used excavator market.

Where is Caterpillar equipment manufactured?

Caterpillar (Irving, Texas — founded 1925) manufactures equipment at 13+ US plants: East Peoria, Illinois (excavators, motor graders); Decatur, Illinois (large mining equipment, large dozers); Mossville, Illinois (engines and powertrain); Aurora, Illinois (small construction equipment); and Victoria, Texas (wheel loaders). Caterpillar employs approximately 55,000 US workers. Despite being a global manufacturer with facilities in 25+ countries, a significant portion of Cat construction equipment sold in North America is manufactured in the United States.

Why does Caterpillar have the best resale value?

Caterpillar's exceptional resale values (68–75% at 3 years) stem from: universal brand recognition (every contractor and lender worldwide knows the yellow CAT), the densest dealer service network in the industry (parts available everywhere quickly), decades of proven durability data, and self-reinforcing demand (high resale makes contractors willing to pay more new, which justifies high resale, in a virtuous cycle). For buyers, this means CAT equipment has the lowest effective ownership cost when you account for both operating costs and resale recovery.
