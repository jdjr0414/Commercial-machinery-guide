Dairy Equipment

Dairy farming has undergone a technology revolution driven by robotic milking, precision herd management, and automated feeding systems. Modern dairy operations require substantial capital investment in specialized equipment with long service lives and direct impact on milk production efficiency.

DeLaval

🇸🇪 Tumba, Sweden (Tetra Laval Group)

World's leading dairy equipment manufacturer. VMS V300 voluntary milking system (robotic), Rotary parlors, parallel parlors, milk cooling tanks. DeLaval Financial Services provides OEM financing. Sold through dealer network across North America.

Lely Industries

🇳🇱 Maassluis, Netherlands

Manufactures the Astronaut A5 robotic milking system — one of the most widely used robotic milking machines globally. Also makes the Juno automatic feed pusher, Discovery manure robot, and Vector automated feeding system. Lely Finance available through dealers.

GEA Farm Technologies

🇩🇪 Bönen, Germany (GEA Group subsidiary)

Major dairy equipment manufacturer. MilkRobot robotic milking, DairyRoter rotary parlors, Swing-Over parlors. GEA Group is publicly traded on Frankfurt Stock Exchange. Strong North American service network.

BouMatic

🇺🇸 Madison, Wisconsin USA

American dairy equipment manufacturer. Robotic milking system, parallel and herringbone parlors, milk meters, automatic cluster removers. One of the few remaining US-headquartered dairy equipment manufacturers. Strong Midwest dealer network.

WW Manufacturing

🇺🇸 Dodge City, Kansas USA

Leading US manufacturer of livestock handling equipment. Squeeze chutes, cattle head gates, working alleys, loading chutes, calf tables. Heavy-duty steel construction. Sold direct and through farm supply dealers nationwide.

🇺🇸 Mount Pleasant, Texas USA

Major US livestock equipment manufacturer. Rod Gates rodeo equipment, cattle squeeze chutes, working systems, feeders. Sold through Southern States, Tractor Supply, and independent dealers. Very accessible financing through commercial ag lenders.

Poultry and Livestock Systems

Commercial poultry operations require specialized environmental control, feeding, and watering equipment. Most poultry growers work under integrator contracts (Tyson, Perdue, Wayne Farms, Pilgrim's Pride) that specify required equipment brands and configurations.

Chore-Time / CTB Inc.

🇺🇸 Milford, Indiana USA (Berkshire Hathaway subsidiary)

Leading US manufacturer of poultry and livestock feeding, watering, and ventilation systems. Owned by Berkshire Hathaway. Chore-Time brand for poultry, Brock for grain systems. Wide integrator acceptance.

Big Dutchman International

🇩🇪 Vechta, Germany (US office Holland, Michigan)

Global leader in poultry and pig production systems. Feeding, drinking, ventilation, and climate systems for broiler, layer, and hog operations. World's largest poultry equipment manufacturer by some measures. Strong integrator relationships in the US market.

Munters Group

🇸🇪 Kista, Sweden (publicly traded)

Leading manufacturer of evaporative cooling and ventilation systems for poultry, swine, and greenhouse applications. AeroCool evaporative cooling systems are widely used in broiler and layer houses in warm climates. Publicly traded on Nasdaq Stockholm.

Roxell

🇧🇪 Maldegem, Belgium (Hendrix Genetics)

Manufactures feeding and drinking systems for broilers, layers, and turkeys. Grill Pro floor feeding, Ronda drinker systems. Hendrix Genetics parent focuses on genetics and poultry systems. Used by integrators in Europe and increasingly in North America.

Trioliet

🇳🇱 Borne, Netherlands

Manufactures TMR (Total Mixed Ration) feed mixing wagons. Solomix vertical and horizontal feed wagons. Widely used in dairy and beef operations. Trioliet equipment is financed through agricultural equipment lenders and Farm Credit systems.

Patz Corporation

American manufacturer of manure handling, feed, and barn equipment. TMR mixers, bunk feeders, push-off silage unloaders. Genuinely American-made. Strong dealer network in dairy states. Accessible financing through commercial ag lenders.

Financing Options for Specialty Agricultural Equipment

Farm Credit System

The Farm Credit System (federally chartered cooperatives including AgCredit, AgFirst, CoBank, etc.) is the largest source of agricultural financing in the US. They understand specialty equipment and offer competitive terms on dairy systems, greenhouse, and precision ag equipment. Farm Credit AgDirect is a popular equipment lending arm.

USDA FSA Loan Guarantees

USDA Farm Service Agency guarantees loans for beginning farmers and operations that can't get conventional credit. Guaranteed Operating Loans (up to $400,000) cover equipment. Guaranteed Farm Ownership Loans (up to $1.776M) cover equipment when purchased with land. Beginning farmer set-asides available.

OEM Manufacturer Financing

DeLaval Financial Services, Lely Finance, GEA Finance, and equipment lenders all offer OEM programs on their ag equipment. These often include 0% promotional periods and deferred payment during seasonal downtime. Strongest for new equipment from dealer channels.

USDA REAP Grants

USDA Rural Energy for America Program (REAP) provides grants and loan guarantees for agricultural energy systems including precision agriculture technology and greenhouse environmental controls. Grants cover up to 25% of project cost. Applications through your local USDA Rural Development office.

Commercial Agricultural Lenders

Rabo AgriFinance, Ag lending divisions of Wells Fargo, Bank of the West (now BMO), and regional agricultural banks provide equipment loans alongside operating lines of credit. Strong relationship with an agricultural lender who knows your operation can unlock better terms than going direct to equipment-only lenders.

Section 179 and Bonus Depreciation

Most agricultural equipment — including robotic milking, poultry systems, and precision ag technology — qualifies for Section 179 immediate expensing (up to $1,160,000 for 2023) and 80% bonus depreciation (2023). See our Section 179 guide. Financed equipment can be expensed in year 1 while payments spread over 60–120 months.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you finance a robotic milking system?

Robotic milking systems (Lely Astronaut A5 from Maassluis Netherlands, DeLaval VMS V300 from Tumba Sweden, GEA MilkRobot from Bönen Germany) cost $150,000–$350,000 per robot unit. Financing is available through manufacturer captive finance programs (Lely Finance, DeLaval Financial Services), Farm Credit AgDirect, and USDA FSA guaranteed loans. Terms run 60–120 months. Most dairy operations finance robotic milkers alongside barn renovation costs through a combined agricultural real estate and equipment loan package structured by their Farm Credit lender.

How much does a dairy milking parlor cost?

A herringbone or parallel milking parlor for a mid-size dairy costs $50,000–$500,000 for equipment alone (milking units, vacuum pump, milk cooling tank, and parlor management system). Rotary parlors for large operations (200+ cows) cost $200,000–$2,000,000+ for equipment. The facility construction — pit, flooring, utilities, and wash systems — adds substantially to total project cost. DeLaval, GEA Farm Technologies, Lely, and BouMatic (Madison, WI) are the primary manufacturers with US dealer networks.

How is poultry house equipment financed?

Poultry house equipment — ventilation, heating, feeding, and watering systems — costs $50,000–$300,000 per house. Most integrators (Tyson, Perdue, Wayne Farms, Pilgrim's Pride) require growers to install equipment meeting specific company specifications. Farm Credit and USDA FSA loan guarantees are the primary financing mechanisms for new poultry houses. Chore-Time/CTB (Milford, IN — Berkshire Hathaway), Big Dutchman (Holland, MI), and Munters (Sweden) are the primary equipment manufacturers with integrator acceptance.

What financing is available for precision agriculture equipment?

Precision agriculture equipment (GPS guidance, yield monitors, variable rate systems, drones) costs $5,000–$80,000 and qualifies for equipment loans through equipment lenders, Farm Credit AgDirect, and commercial banks. equipment lenders seasonal payment programs (no payments during winter) are particularly attractive for row crop farmers. Trimble Agriculture (Westminster, CO), AgLeader Technology (Ames, IA), and Raven Industries (Sioux Falls, SD — now owned by CNH) are leading precision ag providers. Section 179 immediate expensing applies to precision ag equipment.

How do you finance livestock handling equipment?

Livestock handling equipment — squeeze chutes, working alleys, loading chutes, palpation cages — costs $10,000–$150,000 for a complete handling system. These finance through agricultural equipment lenders, Farm Credit, and some commercial banks. WW Manufacturing (Dodge City, KS), Priefert Manufacturing (Mount Pleasant, TX), Hi-Qual Manufacturing (Torrington, WY), and Real Tuff Inc. are primary US brands. Terms: 36–72 months. USDA FSA loan guarantees can help beginning farmers access livestock equipment financing when conventional approval is difficult.
