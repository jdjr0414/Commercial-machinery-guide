Frequently Asked Questions — Hyster-Yale Forklift Financing

Are Hyster and Yale the same forklift?

Essentially yes. Hyster and Yale are sister brands owned by the same parent company — Hyster-Yale Group, Inc. (Cleveland, Ohio — NYSE: HY), which is part of NACCO Industries. They share the same manufacturing platforms: the same machine is produced with either a Hyster badge or a Yale badge, then sold through separate dealer networks. The machines are mechanically identical. The practical advice: choose based on which brand has better dealer/service coverage in your specific area — not on any perceived quality difference between the two badges.

Who owns Hyster-Yale and where are they manufactured?

Hyster-Yale Group, Inc. (Cleveland, Ohio) owns both Hyster and Yale brands. Hyster-Yale is publicly traded on NYSE under ticker HY. Parent company NACCO Industries (Cleveland, Ohio) holds a controlling interest. US manufacturing locations include Greenville, North Carolina; Sulligent, Alabama; and Berea, Kentucky — making Hyster-Yale genuinely American-made equipment.

What is Hyster-Yale Financial Services?

Hyster-Yale Financial Services (HYFS) is the OEM captive financing arm for both Hyster and Yale equipment. HYFS provides standard loans (36–72 months), finance leases, and operating leases through the authorized dealer network. Promotional rates are periodically available. For used Hyster or Yale equipment not purchased through a dealer, independent lenders (ENGS, First Western Equipment Finance, Beacon Capital) readily finance these brands.

What is the most popular Hyster forklift model?

The Hyster H50FT ($22,000–$31,000 new) is the most popular Hyster model — a 5,000 lb LPG cushion tire forklift that is the standard warehouse and distribution forklift for light-to-medium industrial applications. The H40FT (4,000 lb, $20,000–$28,000) is the most popular smaller model for tighter spaces. Both machines are widely available used and have strong lender recognition.

How do Hyster-Yale forklifts compare to Toyota for financing?

Toyota has the #1 forklift market share and highest resale values in the industry — Toyota forklifts consistently bring the most at auction. Hyster and Yale forklifts are priced slightly lower than comparable Toyotas at new ($22,000 vs $25,000 for a 5,000 lb model) and have slightly lower resale values at auction. For financing purposes: Toyota gets slightly better terms from lenders due to collateral strength. However, the price gap often offsets the lower resale for buyers focused on total cost of ownership. Hyster-Yale's US manufacturing in Greenville, NC is a positive factor.

What size Hyster should I finance for a standard warehouse?

For standard warehouse applications with 8-foot pallet racking and typical 2,000–4,000 lb loads: the Hyster H40FT or H50FT (4,000–5,000 lb LPG) is the standard choice for dock loading and indoor/outdoor movement. For warehouse rack reaching (not dock work), a Yale electric reach truck (ERP or MR series) is better. For cold storage or no-emissions environments, the Yale ERP electric series or Hyster J series electric are specified. Match the power type and capacity to the application before financing.
