Licensing & Compliance

Salon, Spa & Med Spa Licensing Requirements

Licensing requirements for beauty services businesses range from simple cosmetology licensing for standard salons to complex medical supervision requirements for med spas. Always verify requirements with your state licensing board and a healthcare attorney before purchasing equipment.

Hair Salon & Cosmetology:

  • State cosmetology license — required in all 50 states for all operators performing cosmetology services
  • Salon/spa facility license — required from state cosmetology board for the business itself (separate from individual operator license)
  • Health department inspection — required in most states for salon facilities
  • Local business license — required in all jurisdictions

Medical Spa & Laser Services:

  • Physician oversight — MD/DO supervision required for laser treatments in most states. Requirements vary by state — some require on-site physician; others allow a medical director agreement. Consult a healthcare attorney in your state before purchasing laser equipment.
  • FDA device compliance — laser and IPL devices are regulated Class II/III medical devices. Ensure your device has FDA 510(k) clearance for the intended application.
  • State-specific laser regulations — some states have explicit laser safety officer requirements and facility inspections for laser-equipped spas.

Tanning Salons:

  • FDA warning labels — required on all tanning beds by federal regulation
  • Minor use restrictions — several states (CA, TX, NY, IL, and others) prohibit or restrict tanning bed use by minors under 18
  • Local fire and building permits — required for facility modifications
  • State tanning facility registration — required in some states

Common Questions

Salon, Spa & Tanning Equipment Financing — FAQ

Do I need a medical license to offer laser hair removal?
The requirement for medical supervision varies significantly by state. In most states, medical-grade laser hair removal (Candela GentleMax Pro, Lumenis LightSheer) requires physician oversight — either a licensed MD/DO performing or directly supervising the treatment, or a licensed laser technician operating under a medical director agreement. States with the strictest requirements (California, New York, Florida, Texas) require that laser treatments be performed by or under the direct supervision of a licensed physician or nurse practitioner. Some states allow licensed estheticians to perform IPL (Intense Pulsed Light) treatments but not true medical-grade laser procedures. Before purchasing any laser system, consult a healthcare attorney in your state. See our Medical Equipment Financing guide for related healthcare equipment.
Can a cosmetologist finance med spa equipment?
A cosmetologist can finance med spa equipment — equipment lenders evaluate creditworthiness and business viability, not scope-of-practice compliance. A cosmetologist with a plan to operate under a medical director agreement (a common model) can typically finance laser equipment with 660+ credit and a demonstrated business plan. Many successful med spa operators are cosmetologists who partner with a supervising physician through a medical director agreement ($1,000–$3,000/month). The Lumenis LightSheer DUET ($45,000–$75,000) or Cutera excel HR ($45,000–$68,000) are common starting points. See our Equipment Financing for Startups guide for new business requirements.
What is the best tanning bed brand for a commercial tanning salon?
Ergoline (Germany) is universally considered the premium commercial tanning bed brand for US salons. The Ergoline 450 ($8,500–$13,500) is the most popular mid-range commercial bed. KBL (Germany) is the second major premium brand with comparable quality. For budget-conscious entry operators, Wolff System (Georgia, USA) offers reliable entry-level beds at $2,500–$4,200. Ergoline and KBL retain significantly better resale value than domestic entry-level brands — an Ergoline 450 purchased new at $10,000 is worth $4,000–$6,000 at 5 years old. Premium German brands are the right choice for any operator planning to operate for 3+ years. See our Section 179 guide for the full deduction available on tanning bed purchases.
How much does a complete med spa build-out cost?
A complete med spa build-out ranges from $150,000 (small single-treatment-room) to $500,000+ (full-service luxury facility). Key cost components: laser equipment ($45,000–$145,000 per device), RF and body contouring devices ($55,000–$115,000 each), interior buildout including treatment rooms ($50,000–$150,000), medical director agreement setup ($5,000–$15,000 legal), practice management software ($5,000–$20,000), initial inventory ($10,000–$30,000), and 3-month working capital reserve ($30,000–$60,000). Most profitable med spas start with 2–3 devices and scale from there. The Candela GentleMax Pro Plus + CoolSculpting Elite combination ($160,000–$240,000) is a popular starter package. See our MRI Machine Financing guide for higher-level medical equipment.
What is the ROI on a Candela GentleMax Pro laser system?
A Candela GentleMax Pro ($65,000–$95,000) generating 6–8 appointments/day at $150–$250 average generates $4,500–$10,000/week — approximately $18,000–$40,000/month at full utilization. Annual revenue from a single GentleMax Pro at 70% utilization: $150,000–$350,000. Equipment financing on an $80,000 GentleMax Pro at 60 months and 7% APR runs approximately $1,584/month. The machine typically pays for itself within 8–18 months. ROI depends heavily on client volume, pricing, and local competition. See our Lease vs. Loan guide for whether leasing or purchasing makes more sense for rapidly-evolving laser technology.
Can I lease salon equipment instead of buying it?
Yes. Salon and spa equipment can be leased or purchased with a loan. Operating leases make sense for medical laser devices that are superseded by newer technology every 3–5 years — a Candela GentleMax Pro Plus on an operating lease at $2,500–$3,500/month for 36 months allows upgrading at lease end. Finance leases (capital leases with $1 buyout) are functionally equivalent to a loan — you own the equipment at the end. For styling chairs, shampoo bowls, and tanning beds — long-lived, stable technology — purchasing is usually better since you build equity. See our full Lease vs. Loan Equipment guide for a complete comparison framework.
How long does commercial tanning equipment last?
Commercial tanning beds from Ergoline and KBL have operational lifespans of 10–15 years with regular maintenance. The primary consumable is the tanning lamp, requiring replacement every 500–1,000 hours ($200–$600 per full bed replacement). Acrylic canopy replacement runs $300–$800 every 3–5 years. A well-maintained Ergoline 450 purchased new retains $3,000–$7,000 in value at 5 years. Entry-level domestic brands have shorter lifespans of 7–10 years and lower resale values. Preventive maintenance contracts for multi-bed locations run $500–$1,500/year. See our Equipment Financing Credit Requirements guide for financing qualification benchmarks.
What credit score do I need to finance a $100,000 laser system?
Financing a $100,000 laser system (Candela GentleMax Pro Plus, Lumenis Stellar M22, Thermage FLX) typically requires 660–680+ personal or business credit for established businesses with 2+ years of history. For borrowers with 700+ credit and strong revenue documentation, 0% down is available for qualified borrowers. Borrowers with 640–660 may need 10–20% down. New med spa operators (startups) generally need 680+ personal credit and a detailed business plan. Terms of 48–72 months are standard for medical equipment at this price point.
