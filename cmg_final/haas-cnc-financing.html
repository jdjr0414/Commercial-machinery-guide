Financing Advantage

Why Haas Is the Easiest CNC Brand to Finance

Among all CNC machine tool brands available in the US market, Haas consistently generates the most favorable financing terms. Three core factors drive this:

  • 100% US Manufacturing: Every Haas machine is built at the 1.1-million-square-foot Haas factory in Oxnard, California. US-made equipment avoids the higher risk premiums lenders assign to imported machines.
  • Largest Installed Base: With 60,000+ Haas machines operating in North America, lenders have deep statistical data on resale values, failure rates, and secondary market demand. This removes uncertainty from the lending decision.
  • Haas Certified Pre-Owned Program: The CPO program (factory-authorized inspection, 1-year warranty) provides clear documentation for used machines, enabling lenders to underwrite used Haas equipment almost as confidently as new. A used VF-2 (3–5 years old) at $28,000–$45,000 is highly liquid collateral.

Common Questions

Haas CNC Financing — FAQ

What are the requirements for Haas Financial Services 0% promotions?
Haas Financial Services' periodic 0% for 24-month promotions require purchasing a new Haas machine through an authorized Haas Factory Outlet (HFO) dealer, with a credit score of approximately 660-680+ and a minimum of 1-2 years in business. The 0% promotion applies to the base machine price on qualifying models during the promotional period. Approval is typically same-day for machines under $150,000 through the HFO dealer integration. When 0% promotions are not active, Haas Financial Services offers fixed-rate programs in the 6-10% APR range for 36-72 month terms.
Can I finance a used Haas CNC machine?
Yes — used Haas CNC machines are among the easiest used machine tools to finance in the US. The Haas Certified Pre-Owned (CPO) program covers machines that pass a factory-authorized inspection and comes with a 1-year warranty, which significantly helps financing approvals. Even non-CPO used Haas machines are well-accepted by specialty equipment lenders because lenders know Haas resale values with precision (60,000+ North American machines create a deep secondary market). A used Haas VF-2 (3-5 years old) typically runs $28,000-$45,000 and can often be financed with 10% down by a shop with 2+ years of history.
Should I finance a Haas VF-2 or VF-4 first?
For a new or growing CNC shop, the VF-2 ($70,000-$90,000 new) is typically the better first finance decision. The VF-2's 30"x16"x20" work envelope handles the majority of commercial milling work, its payment ($1,388-$1,783/month over 60 months at 7%) is manageable with modest customer load, and its resale value is among the most liquid in the market. The VF-4 ($108,000-$138,000) offers a larger table but adds roughly $700-$950/month in payment. Finance the VF-2 first, prove revenue for 12-18 months, then add the VF-4.
Can I take a Section 179 deduction on a Haas CNC machine?
Yes. Haas CNC machining centers, turning centers, and all manufacturing equipment are fully eligible for the Section 179 deduction in the year of purchase. The 2024 Section 179 limit is $1,220,000. A machine shop buying a VF-4SS for $138,000 can deduct the full amount in year one, significantly reducing taxable income. Bonus depreciation provides additional deductions. Section 179 is often the most powerful financial argument for buying rather than leasing a Haas machine. Consult your tax advisor to time the purchase within the tax year.
Can a startup CNC machine shop finance a Haas?
Yes, but with additional requirements. Startup CNC shops (under 2 years) typically need 680+ personal credit, 20-30% down payment, and documentation of machining industry experience (even from prior employment). A signed purchase order or letter of intent from a first customer dramatically improves approval odds. Starting with a used Haas VF-2 or Mini Mill ($25,000-$45,000) is more accessible than financing a new $90,000 machine on day one. Build revenue history for 12-24 months before applying for larger equipment. See our startup equipment financing guide and how to start a CNC machine shop guide.
What is the monthly payment on a Haas VF-2?
A new Haas VF-2 priced at $70,000-$90,000 financed over 60 months at 7% APR runs approximately $1,386-$1,782/month. At 72 months at 7%, payments drop to approximately $1,160-$1,490/month. During Haas Financial Services' 0% for 24-month promotions, a $80,000 VF-2 runs $3,333/month for 24 months (no interest). For a used VF-2 at $35,000-$45,000 financed over 48 months at 8%, payments run approximately $857-$1,100/month. Monthly payment varies significantly by term, rate, and whether 0% promotional financing is available at time of purchase. For business income models, see our CNC machining business income guide.
What is the Haas Certified Pre-Owned program?
The Haas Certified Pre-Owned (CPO) program certifies used Haas machines that pass a comprehensive factory-authorized inspection through an authorized Haas Factory Outlet dealer. CPO machines receive a 1-year limited warranty covering spindle, servo drives, and control components — the most expensive failure points. This warranty significantly improves financing terms from lenders because it reduces the risk of a non-performing asset. CPO machines are typically 2-7 years old and priced at 40-65% of new MSRP. The CPO program is unique to Haas — no other CNC brand offers a comparable program at scale in the US market.
How does Haas compare to Mazak for financing?
Haas is universally easier to finance than Mazak in the US market. Key reasons: Haas is 100% US-manufactured in Oxnard, California, making lenders familiar and comfortable. Haas has the largest installed base of any CNC brand in North America (60,000+ machines) giving lenders precise resale value data. Mazak requires specialist knowledge for lenders and carries higher financing rates. A Haas VF-4 at $108,000-$138,000 is far more accessible than a comparable Mazak Nexus at $140,000-$180,000.
