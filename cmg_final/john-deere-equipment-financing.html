OEM Financing Program

equipment lenders — Program Details

equipment lenders is the captive finance arm of Deere & Company, operating directly through the authorized John Deere dealer network. It is one of the largest agricultural equipment finance companies in the United States. Key programs include:

  • 0% for 48–60 months (ag equipment): Periodic promotional offers on new current-model-year agricultural equipment for qualified buyers (680+ credit).
  • 0% for 24–36 months (construction): Periodic promotional offers on new John Deere construction equipment.
  • Seasonal Payment Structure: Payments aligned to farm revenue cycles — reduced payments during planting and growing seasons, larger payments post-harvest.
  • PowerGard Maintenance Plans: Scheduled maintenance and protection bundled into the financed amount. Covers engine hours or years of operation.
  • Certified Pre-Owned (CPO) Program: Multi-point inspected used John Deere equipment with transferable warranty and competitive financing rates.
  • Fleet Account Programs: Volume financing for contractors and large farming operations purchasing multiple units.

For applications, equipment lenders processes through your authorized dealer. Same-day approvals are common on machines under $150,000. Larger equipment typically closes in 1–3 business days. See also our agricultural equipment financing guide and construction equipment financing guide.

Common Questions

John Deere Equipment Financing — FAQ

How do I qualify for equipment lenders's 0% financing promotion?
equipment lenders's 0% promotional financing on agricultural equipment (typically 0% for 48–60 months) requires a credit score of 680+ and is available only on new current-model-year equipment purchased through an authorized John Deere dealer. Promotions run seasonally — typically in fall and spring at major agricultural events. The zero-percent offer applies to the base machine price; add-ons and attachments may not qualify. You must apply and receive approval through equipment lenders directly at the dealer. Business entity (LLC, S-Corp) purchases may require additional documentation.
How does John Deere's seasonal payment structure work?
equipment lenders's seasonal payment program aligns loan payments to farm cash flow. Rather than equal monthly payments, farmers pay reduced or deferred amounts during planting and pre-harvest months, with larger payments scheduled around harvest time when grain sales generate income. A typical seasonal structure on a $300,000 tractor might require $0 payments in March–May and October, then $9,000–$12,000 payments in November–January to match corn and soybean harvest proceeds. This structure significantly reduces financial stress for row-crop farmers who have lumpy annual income.
Can I finance a used John Deere tractor or combine?
Yes. Used John Deere equipment is among the most financeable used agricultural equipment in the US due to deep lender familiarity and strong resale values. equipment lenders's Certified Pre-Owned (CPO) program covers machines up to 7 years old that pass a multi-point inspection, offering competitive rates with transferable warranty coverage. Third-party ag lenders (Farm Credit, regional banks, specialty equipment lenders) readily finance used John Deere tractors and combines. Typical terms: 48–72 months, 10–15% down, 6–9% APR depending on year and condition.
How does equipment lenders compare to Case IH equipment lenders?
equipment lenders and equipment lenders (Case IH, New Holland) offer comparable OEM financing programs. Key differences: equipment lenders tends to have slightly more aggressive seasonal payment structures for row-crop farmers, while equipment lenders competes aggressively on 0% promotional rates at dealer events. Both offer CPO programs, fleet accounts, and maintenance/warranty bundles. For farmers already in one brand's ecosystem, the OEM lender is usually the best starting point. Independent lenders often beat both on rate but lack seasonal payment flexibility.
Can I finance John Deere construction and agricultural equipment at the same time?
Yes. equipment lenders offers separate lending products for agricultural and construction equipment and can finance both simultaneously. Fleet account programs are available for contractors or farmers purchasing multiple machines. The underwriting looks at total debt service capacity across both loans. For farmers expanding into construction (land clearing, grading), bundling financing through equipment lenders can simplify the process. Third-party lenders can also combine agricultural and construction equipment on a single credit facility.
Can I bundle PowerGard maintenance into my financing?
Yes. John Deere's PowerGard maintenance and extended protection plans can be rolled into the financed amount through equipment lenders. This means the maintenance cost is spread over the loan term rather than paid upfront. PowerGard covers scheduled maintenance and parts for the coverage period — typically 2–5 years or a defined number of engine hours. Bundling makes budgeting predictable but does increase the total financed amount and total interest paid. Evaluate whether the coverage cost is competitive with your dealership's service rates before bundling.
What credit score does equipment lenders require?
equipment lenders's standard programs typically require a personal credit score of 650–680+ for competitive rates. Promotional 0% programs generally require 680+. Established farming operations with strong balance sheets and land equity may qualify even with lower credit scores through the business underwriting track. Newer operators with limited credit history are often evaluated on farm cash flow, land owned, and existing John Deere relationship with the dealer. For scores below 620, third-party specialty lenders or Farm Credit associations are better options.
Can I take the Section 179 deduction on John Deere equipment?
Yes. John Deere tractors, combines, excavators, and other agricultural and construction equipment are fully eligible for the Section 179 deduction in the year of purchase, provided the equipment is used for business purposes 50%+ of the time. The 2024 Section 179 deduction limit is $1,220,000. A farmer financing a $450,000 8R 280 tractor can deduct the full purchase price in year one, dramatically reducing taxable income. Bonus depreciation provides additional first-year deductions. Consult your tax advisor to optimize timing.
