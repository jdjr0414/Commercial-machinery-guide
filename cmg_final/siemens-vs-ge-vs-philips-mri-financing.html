The Big Three 1.5T MRI Systems — What the Price Difference Actually Means

The Siemens MAGNETOM Sola, GE Signa Artist, and Philips Ingenia 1.5T are the flagship current-generation 1.5T MRI systems from the three dominant medical imaging companies. Here is what the price spread between these systems actually reflects:

  • The $80,000 spread between the Siemens Sola ($900K) and GE Artist ($980K) is real but small relative to total project cost
  • Site preparation (RF shielding, structural work, HVAC for the magnet room) adds $200,000–$500,000 to any 1.5T installation
  • Annual service contracts ($60,000–$100,000/year) represent another $600,000–$1,000,000 over a 10-year ownership period
  • The equipment price is only 40–55% of total 10-year cost of ownership
  • This means service quality, service contract terms, and clinical workflow efficiency matter more than the initial price difference

Certificate of Need (CON) — How It Affects Your Financing Decision

CON laws fundamentally change the MRI financing timeline and require specific financing structures. Here is what buyers in CON states need to know:

  • States with CON laws (36 + DC): Including California, New York, Florida, Georgia, Illinois, Virginia, North Carolina, Maryland, Massachusetts, Michigan, Tennessee, South Carolina, Kentucky, and 23 more
  • States WITHOUT CON laws: Texas, Pennsylvania, Ohio, Indiana, Colorado, Idaho, New Mexico, Arizona, South Dakota, North Dakota, Kansas, Montana, Wisconsin, and others — these states allow immediate MRI purchase and installation
  • CON application timeline: 6–18 months typical. Some states (Massachusetts, Maryland) require 12–18 months. Some states have expedited processes for replacement equipment.
  • Financing structure in CON states: Get a conditional commitment letter from your lender (all three OEM programs provide this). Structure a deferred payment program so debt service doesn't begin until after CON approval. Never pay a non-refundable equipment deposit before CON is approved.

Choosing the Right MRI Brand — Decision Framework

After price and financing, the most important decision factors are:

  1. Local service quality: Call 3 imaging centers in your area with each brand and ask about service response time. This matters more than any specification.
  2. Radiologist preference: If you have an affiliated radiologist or will credential radiologists, ask their preference. Workflow familiarity is a genuine productivity factor.
  3. Clinical application focus: Musculoskeletal imaging → any brand. Cardiac → Siemens or GE. Pediatric → Philips (wide bore, quiet modes). Neurological research → Siemens Prisma 3T.
  4. Buy American requirements: Government-affiliated facilities, VA hospitals, or federally-funded health centers may have Buy American preferences → GE Signa (Florence, SC manufacturing)
  5. Helium supply concerns: If helium supply in your region is unreliable or expensive, the Siemens zero-boiloff or Philips BlueSeal technology eliminates this operational risk.

Frequently Asked Questions — Siemens vs GE vs Philips MRI

Which 1.5T MRI brand is least expensive — Siemens, GE, or Philips?

Siemens MAGNETOM Sola is the lowest-priced flagship at $900,000–$1,200,000, compared to Philips Ingenia at $950,000–$1,250,000 and GE Signa Artist at $980,000–$1,280,000. However, the differences are less than 10% and total cost of ownership (including service contracts and helium) matters more than equipment price alone.

Which MRI brand holds its value best?

GE Signa MRI systems hold slightly better US resale at 5 years — 42–52% of new versus Siemens at 40–50% and Philips at 38–48%. GE's US manufacturing and largest hospital installed base support this premium. In the secondary market, all three brands trade within $50,000–$100,000 of each other for comparable age and condition.

Which OEM financing is best for an MRI?

All three OEM programs (equipment lenders, equipment lenders, Philips Healthcare Capital) offer comparable rates for creditworthy borrowers. equipment lenders has the best-structured CON-timeline deferred payment programs. equipment lenders is the largest and most experienced with health system financing. Philips Healthcare Capital is competitive but smaller. Third-party healthcare lenders often compete effectively on rate for all three brands.

Does Philips make a helium-free MRI?

Yes. Philips BlueSeal technology uses a hermetically sealed magnet containing only 7 liters of helium — effectively helium-free in operation. This matches Siemens' zero-boiloff technology on the MAGNETOM Sola. GE's AIR Recharge reduces but doesn't yet fully eliminate helium needs across all current 1.5T models. Helium-free technology eliminates $5,000–$15,000 per refill events and helium supply risk.

How do CON laws affect MRI equipment financing?

CON laws in 36 states require regulatory approval (6–18 months, $15,000–$50,000 in fees) before purchasing or operating an MRI. Never sign a purchase agreement or pay a non-refundable deposit before CON approval. All three OEM lenders provide conditional commitment letters for CON applications. Structure deferred payment programs so debt service doesn't begin until after approval. Texas, Pennsylvania, Ohio, Indiana, and other non-CON states allow immediate purchase.

What are annual service contract costs for each MRI brand?

Annual service contracts for 1.5T MRI systems: Siemens $65,000–$95,000/year, GE $68,000–$98,000/year, Philips $60,000–$92,000/year. Third-party service providers (Agiliti, Shared Medical Services) provide service at 20–40% below OEM costs after warranty. Over 10 years, service contracts typically cost $700,000–$1,000,000 — comparable to the original equipment purchase price.
