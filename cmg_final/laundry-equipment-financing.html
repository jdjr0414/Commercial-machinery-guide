EPA & Regulatory Requirements

Dry Cleaning PERC Regulations & Environmental Permits

Dry cleaning operations using perchloroethylene (PERC) face significant federal, state, and local regulatory requirements. These regulations directly impact equipment financing decisions.

  • 40 CFR Part 63 Subpart M: National Emissions Standard for Hazardous Air Pollutants for existing dry cleaning facilities using PERC. Requires emission controls, leak inspection programs, and recordkeeping.
  • 40 CFR Part 63 Subpart JJJJJ: Requirements for new area source dry cleaning facilities — stricter than Subpart M. New dry cleaners in residential buildings prohibited from using PERC since 2008; phased out at commercial locations starting 2023.
  • California PERC Phase-Out: California has banned all PERC dry cleaning equipment effective January 1, 2023. Operators must transition to hydrocarbon, GreenEarth, or wet cleaning equipment. Other states may follow.
  • State Environmental Permits: Most states require air quality operating permits for PERC users. Annual reporting and emission testing required.
  • Fire Department Hazmat Permits: PERC and hydrocarbon solvents require local fire department hazardous materials storage permits and annual inspections.
  • Wastewater Discharge Permits: Laundry operations discharge significant wastewater. Industrial pretreatment permits required in many municipalities for laundries above certain volume thresholds.
  • Building / HVAC Ventilation Permits: Dry cleaning operations require specialized ventilation systems to meet OSHA PEL for PERC (25 ppm 8-hr TWA). Building permits required for installation.
  • Vented Laundry Emissions: Coin laundromats with natural gas dryers may need air quality permits in certain air basin management areas (particularly California's SJV and SoCal AQMDs).

Common Questions

Commercial Laundry Equipment Financing — FAQ

What is the monthly payment for a coin laundry buildout?
A small coin laundromat with 15–20 machines costs approximately $60,000–$120,000 in equipment. Financed over 60 months at 7% APR, monthly payments run $1,188–$2,376. A mid-size laundromat (25–35 machines) at $150,000–$250,000 runs $2,970–$4,950/month. A large vended laundry with 50+ machines at $350,000–$600,000 runs $6,930–$11,880/month. Equipment financing typically covers 80–90% of machine cost; leasehold improvements and utility hookups are typically funded separately.
How do EPA PERC regulations affect dry cleaning equipment financing?
Some lenders are cautious about financing PERC equipment due to future regulatory risk and environmental liability. California eliminated PERC dry cleaning in 2023, and other states may follow. Hydrocarbon and GreenEarth alternative solvent machines are easier to finance and have better long-term collateral value. 40 CFR Part 63 Subparts M and JJJJJ impose ongoing compliance costs that lenders weigh when evaluating dry cleaning financing applications. Environmental remediation liability from PERC can make some lenders reluctant entirely.
Should I lease or buy commercial laundry equipment?
For coin laundromats planning to operate 7+ years, buying (financing) is almost always superior. Commercial washers and dryers from Speed Queen and Dexter last 15–25 years — far outlasting any lease term. You build equity and take Section 179 deductions. Operating leases work better for on-premise hotel or hospital laundry wanting to refresh equipment on 5–7 year cycles. See our full equipment financing vs. lease guide.
What are the best brands for laundry equipment financing?
Speed Queen, Dexter, Huebsch, and Milnor are the most readily financed brands. All have active secondary markets and documented resale values. Speed Queen (Alliance Laundry Systems) is the dominant US brand for vended laundry. For institutional (150 lb+) equipment, Milnor, Girbau, and Jensen are recognized by specialty lenders. European brands require lenders familiar with the segment but are financeable with appropriate documentation.
How do I finance a coin laundry startup?
Coin laundry startups can be financed through specialty equipment lenders or SBA 7(a) loans. Requirements typically include: 650+ personal credit, 10–20% down payment, location lease signed, and utility capacity confirmed. SBA 7(a) loans allow bundling equipment + leasehold improvements + working capital into one loan. Several specialty lenders have dedicated coin laundry programs with streamlined approval for Speed Queen and Dexter equipment.
What credit score is required for laundry equipment financing?
Most equipment lenders require 640–660+ for established laundry businesses and 680+ for startups. Coin laundry equipment (Speed Queen, Dexter) is among the easiest commercial equipment to finance due to exceptional resale values. Alternative lenders work with 550–620+ for established operators with strong bank statements. See our equipment financing credit requirements guide.
How much does it cost to start a coin laundry?
A small coin laundromat (15–20 machines, 1,200–1,800 sq ft) in existing space costs $80,000–$200,000 total: equipment ($50,000–$120,000), leasehold improvements ($15,000–$50,000), permits and utility hookups ($5,000–$20,000), and working capital ($10,000–$20,000). A mid-size facility (25–40 machines) costs $200,000–$450,000. A large modern laundromat with premium machines costs $400,000–$800,000+.
How does tunnel washer financing work?
Tunnel washers (continuous batch washers or CBW) range from $500,000 to $3,000,000 and require lenders with industrial laundry expertise. Financing requires 0–20% down depending on credit (0% available for qualified borrowers), 60–84 month terms. Lenders want 2–3 years of business financial statements, institutional customer contracts (hospitals, hotels, linen services), and demonstrated revenue of $2M+. Jensen Group, Milnor CBW, and Kannegiesser offer OEM financing programs for qualified operators. See our equipment financing guide for large-ticket equipment strategies.
