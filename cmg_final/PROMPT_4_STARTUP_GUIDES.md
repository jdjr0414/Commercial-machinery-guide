## PAGE 1: how-to-start-cnc-machine-shop.html

Title: How to Start a CNC Machine Shop | Complete Startup Guide — Costs, Equipment, Licensing
Meta: Complete guide to starting a CNC machine shop. Equipment costs, required licenses, shop layout, finding customers, staffing, and financing. Total startup cost: $150,000–$800,000 for a basic job shop.
H1: How to Start a CNC Machine Shop

Quick Answer: Starting a basic CNC job shop requires $150,000–$800,000 total investment covering 1-3 CNC machines, tooling, workholding, metrology equipment, and working capital. No specific machinist license is required in most states, but OSHA machine guarding compliance is mandatory. The most important factor is customer acquisition — most new shops succeed by working as subcontractors for established manufacturers before pursuing direct contracts.

Content sections:

STEP 1 — DEFINE YOUR NICHE (400 words):
- Job shop vs production shop — key differences in equipment needs and business model
- Industries to consider: aerospace/defense (highest margins, hardest to enter), medical devices (growing, requires ISO 13485), automotive tier 2/3, general manufacturing
- Material specialization: steel, aluminum, titanium, exotic alloys, plastics — your equipment and tooling choices depend on this
- Part complexity: simple parts on basic VMCs vs complex 5-axis parts — don't buy 5-axis if you can't program it
- Volume: low-volume/high-mix vs high-volume production — determines automation investment

STEP 2 — EQUIPMENT SELECTION AND TOTAL COST (500 words):
Minimum viable CNC job shop:
- 1 CNC VMC (Haas VF-2 or similar, used): $25,000-$55,000
- 1 CNC Lathe (Haas ST-20 or similar, used): $20,000-$45,000
- CAD/CAM software (Mastercam, Fusion 360): $5,000-$15,000/year
- Tooling, inserts, holders: $10,000-$20,000 initial
- Workholding (vises, fixtures, chucks): $5,000-$15,000
- Metrology (calipers, micrometers, CMM or height gauge): $5,000-$25,000
- Safety equipment, PPE: $2,000-$5,000
- Total minimum viable: $72,000-$180,000

Proper 2-machine startup:
- 1 new Haas VF-2: $72,000-$90,000
- 1 new Haas ST-20: $48,000-$62,000
- Full tooling package: $25,000-$40,000
- Workholding: $15,000-$25,000
- CAD/CAM: $10,000-$20,000
- CMM (used Mitutoyo or Zeiss): $15,000-$40,000
- Compressed air system: $3,000-$8,000
- Coolant system (included with machines)
- Working capital: $30,000-$60,000
- Total: $218,000-$345,000

STEP 3 — FACILITY REQUIREMENTS (300 words):
- Square footage: 1,000-3,000 sq ft per machine minimum (including material storage, work area, office)
- Floor: concrete slab minimum 6 inches thick (machines can weigh 10,000-30,000 lbs)
- Power: 3-phase 208V or 480V required — check facility before signing lease
- Compressed air: shop air at 90-120 PSI minimum
- Crane/forklift: needed for loading heavy parts; a $15,000-$25,000 shop crane or forklift access
- Lighting: bright LED lighting throughout (3,000 lux minimum at work surfaces)
- Ventilation: coolant mist extraction required (OSHA requirement); $5,000-$15,000 for mist collectors

STEP 4 — LICENSING AND COMPLIANCE (300 words):
- Business license (state and local)
- OSHA 29 CFR 1910.212 (machine guarding — mandatory)
- OSHA 29 CFR 1910.147 (lockout/tagout — mandatory)
- EPA coolant disposal compliance
- Hazardous waste permit if generating over threshold
- ISO 9001: not legally required but expected by aerospace/medical/automotive customers ($10,000-$25,000 to certify)
- AS9100D: required for aerospace (builds on ISO 9001, stricter) — $15,000-$35,000 to certify
- ISO 13485: required for medical device manufacturing
- ITAR registration: required if working on defense items ($2,500 registration fee)

STEP 5 — FINDING YOUR FIRST CUSTOMERS (400 words):
- Thomasnet.com: industrial buyer directory, list your capabilities
- MFG.com and Xometry: job shop marketplaces where OEMs post RFQs
- Local manufacturing associations
- Cold calling tier 2/3 suppliers in your area
- Subcontracting to larger shops (common way to start)
- Government contracting (SAM.gov registration, NAICS codes)
- Machine shop brokers and sourcing agents

Income potential: single machine ($100K-$250K revenue), 2-machine shop ($250K-$600K), 4-6 machine job shop ($600K-$1.5M), established shop 10+ machines ($2M-$8M+)

FAQ (6 questions): startup cost, licenses required, how to find first customers, new vs used machines for startups, how long to profitability, ISO certification — when do you need it

Meta: Complete guide to starting a commercial equipment rental business. Fleet selection, required licenses, utilization rates, pricing strategy, and what rental companies earn. Small fleet startup: $200K–$1M.
H1: How to Start an Equipment Rental Business

Quick Answer: Starting a small equipment rental business requires $200,000–$1,000,000 for an initial fleet of 10-20 pieces of equipment. Typical utilization rates (days rented per year) should be 60-75% to be profitable. Daily rental rates should be approximately 1/200 of equipment cost for daily rates, 1/50 for weekly.
