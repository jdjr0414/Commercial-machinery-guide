Trane Technologies — Company Background and US Manufacturing

Trane Technologies plc (Dublin, Ireland) was created in 2020 when Ingersoll-Rand plc split into two companies: Trane Technologies (climate control products) and the new Ingersoll Rand Inc. (industrial equipment). US operations are headquartered in Davidson, North Carolina. Manufacturing facilities for commercial HVAC include Clarksville, Tennessee (large commercial RTUs), La Crosse, Wisconsin (centrifugal chillers), and Pueblo, Colorado (unit heaters and controls).

The Trane brand was founded in La Crosse, Wisconsin in 1913 by Reuben Trane. The company invented the CenTraVac centrifugal chiller in 1931, establishing the hermetic low-pressure refrigerant technology that defines the product to this day. Trane Technologies employs approximately 40,000 people and sells under the Trane brand in North America and the Thermo King brand for transport refrigeration globally.

Trane as a Service — The Equipment-as-a-Service Alternative to Financing

Trane Technologies has developed a managed services offering called "Trane as a Service" that represents an alternative to traditional equipment financing. Under this model:

  • Trane installs and owns the HVAC equipment
  • The facility pays a fixed monthly service fee covering equipment, maintenance, and performance guarantees
  • Energy performance is contractually guaranteed — if the system underperforms, Trane covers the difference
  • At contract end (typically 10–15 years), the facility can buy the equipment, extend the contract, or have Trane upgrade to new equipment

This model is particularly attractive for facilities with limited capital budgets, non-profit organizations, or facilities seeking off-balance-sheet treatment. It is most commonly used in K-12 schools, municipalities, and healthcare systems.

Trane Technologies Financial Services — Financing Programs

Trane Technologies Financial Services provides dedicated HVAC financing with structures tailored to commercial facilities:

  • Equipment Loans: 36–84 month fixed rate loans for commercial RTUs and chillers. Best for facilities that want straightforward ownership.
  • Operating Lease: Monthly payments with FMV buyout at end of term. Equipment stays off balance sheet — benefits organizations with restrictive debt covenants.
  • Energy Performance Contracting: Multi-year contracts where energy savings guarantee debt service. Used frequently in K-12 schools and municipalities that cannot add debt to their balance sheets.
  • PACE Financing (where available): Property Assessed Clean Energy allows commercial HVAC upgrades to be repaid through property tax over 10–25 years. Available in 36+ states through Trane's approved PACE lending partners.

Frequently Asked Questions — Trane HVAC Financing

How much does a Trane commercial rooftop unit cost?

Trane commercial RTUs (Voyager, Precedent, IntelliPak) range from $6,000–$20,000 for small commercial units (3–10 ton) to $115,000–$295,000 for large IntelliPak units (50–130 ton). The most common restaurant and retail sizes (10–25 ton) cost $20,000–$50,000 new. Trane carries a slight price premium over Carrier and York, justified by the IntelliPak controls system and service quality.

How much does a Trane CenTraVac chiller cost?

The Trane CenTraVac centrifugal chiller ranges from $350,000 (smaller 150-ton CVHE configurations) to $2.5 million or more for large capacity CVHF units. The CenTraVac is renowned for 30–40-year service life. Used CenTraVac chillers in working condition typically sell for $120,000–$875,000 depending on age, capacity, and service history.

Does Trane offer equipment financing?

Yes. Trane Technologies Financial Services provides OEM financing for commercial HVAC with 36–84 month terms. Trane also offers "Trane as a Service" — a managed services model where facilities pay monthly fees covering equipment, maintenance, and performance guarantees rather than purchasing outright. This model is popular with schools, municipalities, and healthcare systems with limited capital budgets.

What is the Trane IntelliPak controls system?

Trane IntelliPak is the advanced controls platform used in Trane's large commercial rooftop units. It provides diagnostics, building automation system integration, demand-controlled ventilation, and remote monitoring. IntelliPak is widely considered the best RTU controls system available, and diagnostic data savings alone justify the Trane price premium for many facilities managers over a 15–20 year equipment life.

What is the Trane CGAM and how much does it cost?

The Trane CGAM is an air-cooled scroll chiller for commercial buildings (20–300 ton). CGAM pricing ranges from $45,000–$95,000 for smaller units (20–75 ton) to $185,000–$485,000 for larger configurations (150–300 ton). It uses R-410A or R-32 refrigerant and is commonly specified for commercial office buildings, retail centers, and light industrial facilities where cooling tower water treatment is not practical.

How does Trane HVAC financing compare to Carrier?

Trane Technologies Financial Services and Carrier Financial Services offer comparable financing structures and rates. Trane's unique advantage is the Trane as a Service model and energy performance contracting — structures not broadly offered by Carrier. For straightforward equipment purchases, both offer similar 36–84 month terms. For large chiller plant projects, Trane's dedicated project finance team can structure multi-year payment programs aligned with expected energy savings.

What makes CenTraVac chillers last so long?

The Trane CenTraVac uses a hermetically sealed, direct-drive centrifugal compressor operating at atmospheric pressure with low-pressure refrigerant R-123. This eliminates shaft seals (a common failure point on other chillers) and prevents refrigerant leaks. Oil-free designs in newer CenTraVac models eliminate oil fouling on heat transfer surfaces. Trane's Adaptive Control prevents the compressor from operating outside safe parameters. The result: documented 35+ year CenTraVac installations in hospitals and university central plants nationwide.
