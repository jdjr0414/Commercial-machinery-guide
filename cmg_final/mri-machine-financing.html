Certificate of Need Laws

CON Laws by State — MRI Approval Requirements

Certificate of Need (CON) laws regulate major medical equipment purchases in many states. Purchasing an MRI in a CON state without approval results in civil penalties and loss of CMS reimbursement eligibility. Lenders will not fund MRI purchases in CON states without documentation of CON approval or a valid exemption.

CON applications cost $2,000–$10,000 and take 3–12 months to process.

ACR Accreditation

ACR MRI Accreditation — Required for CMS Reimbursement

The American College of Radiology (ACR) MRI accreditation program is required for Medicare and Medicaid reimbursement for outpatient MRI services under the Deficit Reduction Act of 2005. Without ACR accreditation, CMS will not reimburse MRI scans at an outpatient imaging center — this is a non-negotiable revenue prerequisite.

  • Phantom Testing: Standard ACR phantom must be scanned and results submitted for image quality (SNR, ghosting, geometric accuracy, slice thickness, resolution).
  • Medical Physicist Survey: Licensed medical physicist must perform site survey, review equipment specifications, and sign off on phantom results. Cost: $2,000–$5,000.
  • Personnel Qualifications: Radiologist medical director must meet ACR board certification requirements. MRI technologists must be ARRT (MR) or ARMRIT certified.
  • Initial Accreditation Cost: Approximately $1,800 application fee plus physicist fees. Annual maintenance fees apply.
  • Timeline: 3–6 months from application to accreditation. Plan this into your imaging center opening timeline and financing projections.
  • Site Documentation: Written quality control program, equipment service records, personnel training logs.

Common Questions

MRI Machine Financing — FAQ

What is a Certificate of Need (CON) law for MRI?
CON laws require healthcare facilities to obtain state government approval before purchasing major medical equipment like MRI scanners. 35+ states have CON laws. Applications cost $2,000–$10,000 and take 3–12 months. Purchasing an MRI without required CON approval results in civil penalties and may void CMS reimbursement. Lenders will not fund MRI purchases in CON states without documented CON approval or a clear exemption.
What is the monthly payment on a 1.5T MRI machine?
A new 1.5T MRI at $800,000–$1,200,000 financed over 84 months at 6.5% APR runs approximately $12,093–$18,139/month. A refurbished 1.5T at $200,000–$500,000 financed over 72 months at 7.5% runs approximately $3,437–$8,592/month. Site preparation adds $150,000–$400,000. Annual OEM service contracts add $80,000–$200,000/year — factor this into cash flow projections. See Siemens Healthineers financing and GE Healthcare financing for OEM programs.
Should I buy refurbished or new MRI equipment?
Refurbished MRI systems offer 40–60% savings: a refurbished 1.5T runs $150,000–$600,000 vs. $500,000–$1,500,000 new. Trade-offs: older software, shorter OEM service contract availability. For routine community imaging (brain, spine, extremity), refurbished 1.5T is often the right choice. For specialty sequences or hospital-level use, newer software is often essential. See our full Siemens vs. GE vs. Philips MRI financing comparison.
What is helium-free BlueSeal MRI technology?
Philips Ingenia Ambition/Elition BlueSeal and Siemens Magnetom Altea use sealed helium systems requiring only 0.7 liters of liquid helium vs. 1,500–2,000 liters for conventional MRI. This eliminates $5,000–$15,000/year in helium refill costs and reduces quench risk. BlueSeal systems cost $100,000–$300,000 more than conventional equivalents but reduce 10-year operating costs significantly. Lenders view BlueSeal positively as it reduces operational risk.
What are ACR accreditation requirements for MRI?
ACR MRI accreditation requires phantom testing, medical physicist review ($2,000–$5,000), documentation of personnel qualifications, and a written quality control program. Initial cost: ~$1,800 application fee + physicist fees. Timeline: 3–6 months. CMS will not reimburse outpatient MRI at non-accredited facilities — this is a hard revenue prerequisite that must be planned into your imaging center opening timeline.
Can a startup imaging center get MRI financing?
Yes, but it is challenging. Requirements typically include: 700+ personal credit, 25–30% down payment, physician ownership or committed medical director, CON approval (if applicable), ACR accreditation timeline plan, and 3-year financial projections. SBA 7(a) loans are commonly used. See our MRI imaging center startup guide and startup equipment financing guide.
Can I get MRI financing with bad credit?
MRI financing with credit below 650 is difficult due to transaction size ($300K–$3.5M+). Options: add a co-borrower with stronger credit, increase down payment to 30–40%, use a lease structure, consider a refurbished system, or use SBA 504 which weighs business financials heavily. Healthcare equipment lenders will also evaluate CMS reimbursement contracts and practice revenue as mitigants to personal credit weakness.
What is the difference between a 1.5T and 3T MRI?
Tesla (T) refers to magnetic field strength. 3T has double the field strength of 1.5T, producing higher resolution images and faster scan times. 3T advantages: superior soft tissue contrast, faster throughput, better fMRI and spectroscopy. 3T disadvantages: higher cost ($1M–$3.5M vs. $500K–$1.5M for 1.5T), more metal artifact issues, higher SAR limitations. For community imaging centers doing routine brain, spine, and body MRI, 1.5T is sufficient and more economical.
