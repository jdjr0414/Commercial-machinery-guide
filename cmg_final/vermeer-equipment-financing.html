Financing Advantage

Why Vermeer's American Manufacturing Matters for Financing

Vermeer Corporation was founded in 1948 by Gary Vermeer in Pella, Iowa. Today it remains family-owned and privately held — now led by the third generation of the Vermeer family. The company is not publicly traded, meaning all financial decisions are made in Iowa rather than by investors seeking quarterly returns.

This ownership structure and manufacturing origin have concrete financing implications:

  • 100% US Manufacturing: Every Vermeer product — from the BC600XL wood chipper to the D1000x900 HDD drill — is manufactured in Pella, Iowa. US-made equipment avoids the risk premiums lenders assign to imported machinery.
  • Established Resale Market: Vermeer equipment has decades of US resale history. Lenders can price collateral values with confidence, resulting in better terms.
  • Vermeer Financial Services: The OEM captive lender understands Vermeer equipment values precisely and integrates directly with the dealer network for efficient approvals.
  • Dealer Network: Nationwide Vermeer dealer coverage means service support — a factor lenders consider when evaluating equipment value retention.

Common Questions

Vermeer Equipment Financing — FAQ

Vermeer vs Morbark vs Bandit — which is easiest to finance?
Vermeer is generally the easiest of the three to finance due to its larger dealer network, broader brand recognition among equipment lenders, and Vermeer Financial Services' established OEM program. All three brands are American-made, which is a financing advantage over imported alternatives. Bandit Industries (Remus, Michigan) and Morbark (Winn, Michigan) are both well-regarded by specialty forestry equipment lenders, but major national banks and credit unions are more familiar with Vermeer by volume. For practical financing purposes, all three are accessible through specialty ag/forestry lenders with 650+ credit and 10-20% down.
What is the monthly payment on a Vermeer BC1800XL chipper?
A new Vermeer BC1800XL 18-inch chipper priced at $85,000-$165,000 financed over 60 months at 7% APR runs approximately $1,683-$3,267/month. At 72 months at 7%, payments drop to approximately $1,409-$2,733/month. The wide price range reflects configuration options — engine choice, feed system, and trailer type all affect final price. Used BC1800XL units (3-5 years old) at $45,000-$85,000 financed over 48 months at 8% run approximately $1,100-$2,079/month. Tree service operators should model payment against typical day-rate billing of $800-$2,500/day to ensure coverage.
How do I finance a new HDD rig for a utility contractor?
Financing a new Vermeer Navigator HDD for utility contractor work follows construction equipment lending standards. Lenders look for: 2+ years in business, 650+ personal credit, signed contracts or work history documentation, and typically 10-15% down on established operations. Larger drills ($300,000+) may require 15-20% down for newer operators. The Navigator D23x30 ($55,000-$75,000) is a common entry-level utility HDD and is straightforward to finance. For large D550x500 or D1000x900 drills ($700,000-$1.5M+), specialty infrastructure equipment lenders with HDD experience are recommended.
How does Vermeer Financial compare to third-party lenders?
Vermeer Financial Services (the OEM captive lender) offers the advantage of equipment-specific expertise and direct dealer integration for fast approvals. They offer periodic promotional rates and understand Vermeer resale values precisely. Third-party specialty lenders can sometimes offer lower rates, especially for well-qualified buyers, and offer more flexibility on term length and structure. For most buyers, getting a quote from Vermeer Financial alongside a third-party specialty lender is the optimal approach — OEM promotions sometimes win, and third-party lenders sometimes beat OEM rates on standard programs.
Can I finance used Vermeer equipment?
Yes. Used Vermeer equipment — particularly BC series chippers, SC stump grinders, and Navigator HDD drills — is well-accepted by specialty forestry and construction equipment lenders. Vermeer's American manufacturing and strong resale market provide lenders with confidence in collateral values. Typical terms on used Vermeer: 48-60 months, 10-15% down, 7-10% APR depending on age and condition. Machines over 10 years old may require higher down payments or shorter terms. Lenders generally prefer machines under 7 years old for best terms.
Can a startup tree service finance Vermeer equipment?
Yes, with the right preparation. New tree service businesses (under 2 years) typically need 680+ personal credit, 20-30% down, and documentation of arborist experience (certifications, prior employment). Starting with a used Vermeer BC900XL or BC1000XL ($20,000-$35,000 used) is much more accessible than a new BC1800XL. A signed contract from a property manager or municipality significantly improves approval odds. Build 12-24 months of revenue history before applying for a $100,000+ machine. See our how to start a tree service business guide and tree service licensing requirements guide.
Is Vermeer equipment made in the USA?
Yes. Vermeer Corporation manufactures all of its products at its facilities in Pella, Iowa. The company was founded in 1948 by Gary Vermeer and remains family-owned — now led by the third generation of the Vermeer family. Vermeer is not publicly traded, meaning financial decisions are made in Iowa, not by Wall Street shareholders. This 100% American manufacturing heritage is a genuine financing advantage: lenders apply lower risk premiums to US-manufactured equipment with established domestic resale markets, compared to imported alternatives.
Can I take a Section 179 deduction on Vermeer equipment?
Yes. Vermeer chippers, stump grinders, trenchers, HDD drills, and other business equipment are fully eligible for the Section 179 deduction in the year of purchase. The 2024 Section 179 limit is $1,220,000. A tree service purchasing a $95,000 BC1800XL chipper can deduct the full cost in year one, dramatically reducing taxable income. Bonus depreciation provides additional first-year deductions. See our tree service business income guide for revenue modeling.
