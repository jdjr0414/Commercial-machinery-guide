Manufacturer Profile

About Fecon Inc. — Lebanon, Ohio

🇺🇸 Lebanon, Ohio

Fecon Inc.

Founded in 1992, Fecon Inc. is headquartered and manufactures in Lebanon, Ohio — about 30 miles northeast of Cincinnati. Fecon is a privately held company that has focused exclusively on forestry mulching and land clearing equipment since its founding. Their product focus (mulchers only) has made them a specialist brand highly respected in the ROW clearing and land clearing industries. All Fecon equipment is manufactured in the USA.

🇺🇸 US Manufacturing

Manufacturing & Distribution

Fecon's Lebanon, Ohio facility handles design, engineering, and manufacturing. The company sells through a dealer network across North America and directly to large ROW contractors and utilities. Fecon's US manufacturing base is a significant advantage for financing — lenders treat American-made equipment more favorably, and parts availability is generally superior to imported alternatives.

🏭 Product Focus

Product Lines

Fecon produces four main product lines: Bull Hog skid steer/CTL attachments, FTX dedicated tracked mulchers, TK tractor-mounted mulchers, and RK excavator mulchers. This exclusive focus on mulching equipment — rather than a broad equipment catalog — has made Fecon the brand of choice for professional land clearing operations. Fecon's Bite Limiter technology on rotor teeth is a recognized innovation in the industry.

Common Questions

Fecon Equipment Financing — FAQ

Fecon vs FAE vs Rayco — which forestry mulcher is best?
Fecon, FAE, and Rayco are the top three dedicated forestry mulcher brands in North America. Fecon (Lebanon, Ohio) is the American choice, made in the USA with strong dealer and parts support. FAE (Italian-made, North American distribution) is often considered the premium option with slightly higher prices and excellent build quality. Rayco (Wooster, Ohio) also manufactures in the USA and is well-regarded for its complete stump grinders and forestry mulchers. For financing, all three are well-known to specialty lenders. Fecon's US manufacturing gives it a slight advantage with American lenders assessing collateral value.
Can I finance a Fecon FTX tracked mulcher as a startup?
Yes, startup financing for Fecon FTX tracked mulchers is possible but requires preparation. Expect 20–30% down payment for new businesses (under 2 years), a personal credit score of 680+, and ideally a signed contract or letter of intent from your first customer. A startup plan demonstrating target markets — utility ROW contracts, municipal land clearing, real estate development — strengthens the application. FTX tracked mulchers ($85,000–$420,000) are specialty equipment, so use lenders that know the land clearing category: AgDirect, Farm Credit, or commercial equipment finance companies. Starting with a used FTX75L or FTX100L at $45,000–$80,000 reduces startup financing risk significantly.
What is the monthly payment on a Fecon FTX148L?
A new Fecon FTX148L (148 HP tracked forestry mulcher) costs approximately $235,000–$320,000. At $275,000 financed over 60 months at 7.5% APR, monthly payments run approximately $5,493/month. At 72 months at 8%, payments drop to approximately $4,765/month. For established land clearing contractors with strong revenue and good credit, rates in the 6.5–7.5% range are realistic. What is the income potential for a land clearing business?
Land clearing income varies significantly by market and machine type. A solo operator with a single tracked mulcher (FTX128L or larger) typically bills $2,500–$5,000 per day, generating $150,000–$400,000 in annual revenue with owner income of $60,000–$150,000 after equipment payments and expenses. A two-machine operation generates $350,000–$800,000. The highest-value work is right-of-way (ROW) clearing for utilities — pipeline companies, electric utilities, and municipalities. Established ROW contractors with 3–5 machines and utility contracts can generate $300,000–$1,200,000+ annually.
How do I get right-of-way utility contracts for land clearing?
Getting utility ROW contracts typically requires: 1) 1–2 years operating experience with a documented safety record, 2) General Liability insurance at $1M–$2M per occurrence (utilities often require $5M), 3) ISNetworld or Avetta registration (most major utilities require this), 4) Ability to post performance bonds for larger contracts, 5) A fleet capable of handling the volume. Start by subcontracting to established ROW contractors to build experience. Contact the ROW procurement departments at regional electric cooperatives and pipeline companies directly. State DOT corridor maintenance contracts are also a good entry point for newer operations.
Fecon Bull Hog for skid steer vs a dedicated Fecon FTX mulcher — which should I buy first?
If you already own a high-flow skid steer or CTL, the Bull Hog attachment ($12,000–$32,000) is the lower-risk entry point into land clearing. The attachment leverages equipment you already own, the financing is simple, and you can assess market demand before committing to a $100,000+ dedicated machine. The limitation: skid steer mulchers are slower and less productive than dedicated tracked mulchers. For serious land clearing volume — ROW work, large commercial site clearing — a dedicated FTX machine is far more productive. If land clearing is your primary business, a dedicated FTX tracked mulcher is almost always the right long-term answer. If it's supplementary work on top of existing operations, start with the Bull Hog.
What are the maintenance costs for a Fecon forestry mulcher?
Forestry mulchers are high-wear machines with significant ongoing maintenance. The main ongoing expense is rotor teeth and carbide bits — a set of carbide teeth for a Bull Hog or FTX mulcher costs $800–$3,500 per set, with replacement frequency varying from weekly (rocky or abrasive conditions) to monthly (normal conditions). Annual maintenance budget for a heavily used FTX tracked mulcher running 1,000+ hours: $12,000–$25,000 including teeth, belts, hydraulic services, and engine maintenance. Budget 8–12% of purchase price annually for maintenance on heavily used machines. Proper technique — not rushing through rocky soil — dramatically extends tooth life.
Can I finance a used Fecon mulcher?
Yes. Used Fecon mulcher financing is available through specialty equipment lenders. Lenders typically finance machines up to 10–12 years old in good operating condition. A used Fecon FTX128L (5–7 years old) typically sells for $90,000–$140,000 at auction or through dealers. Used Bull Hog attachments in good condition sell for $6,000–$18,000. For used Fecon FTX machines, expect 15–20% down with specialty lenders. An independent equipment appraisal ($300–$600) can help with lender approval for older or high-hour machines. IronPlanet and Ritchie Bros. auction results provide useful comparable sales data when approaching lenders.
