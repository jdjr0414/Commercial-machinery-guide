OSHA Requirements

OSHA Standards Every Manufacturing Facility Must Follow

OSHA compliance is mandatory for all US manufacturing employers. The following standards are the most relevant and most frequently cited for CNC shops, metal fabrication, and general manufacturing. Penalties for serious violations: up to $15,625 per violation; willful violations: up to $156,259.

Electrical Permits

3-Phase Power & Electrical Permits for CNC Equipment

CNC machining centers, laser cutters, press brakes, and most industrial manufacturing equipment require 3-phase electrical power — which is not available in most residential areas and may not be at your intended commercial location. Planning the electrical installation is a critical part of any manufacturing startup.

  • Voltage Requirements: Most CNC machining centers require 3-phase 208V or 480V service. Larger equipment (laser cutters, wire EDM, injection molding machines) may require 480V 3-phase at 100–400 amps.
  • Licensed Electrician Required: All US states require licensed (master electrician) sign-off on commercial electrical installations. Permits must be pulled before work begins.
  • Building Permit: A building permit is required for any new electrical service, subpanel, or circuit installation. Inspections by the AHJ (authority having jurisdiction) are required before energizing.
  • Transformers: Where 3-phase power is unavailable from the utility, rotary phase converters or solid-state phase converters can synthesize 3-phase from single-phase service. Cost: $2,000–$15,000 depending on load. Note that phase converters may void some machine warranties — verify with the OEM.
  • Service Upgrade Cost: Upgrading a commercial space from 200A single-phase to 400A or 800A 3-phase service costs $5,000–$30,000 depending on distance from transformer and local utility requirements.

ISO 9001 is a quality management system standard — it certifies that your processes are documented, controlled, and continuously improved. It does not certify product quality directly, but demonstrates you have systems to produce consistent quality. Most automotive and industrial customers require it for any supplier receiving purchase orders.

AS9100 (current revision: AS9100D) adds aerospace-specific requirements on top of ISO 9001: first article inspection (FAI) per AS9102, foreign object damage (FOD) prevention, risk management, key characteristics flow-down, and configuration management. Required for all aerospace supply chain suppliers. Certification bodies must be IAQG-recognized (BSI, DNV GL, SGS, Bureau Veritas, etc.).

ITAR Registration

ITAR — International Traffic in Arms Regulations

ITAR (22 CFR Parts 120–130) controls the export, import, and transfer of defense articles listed on the US Munitions List (USML). Manufacturing shops that machine, fabricate, or provide services for USML items must register with DDTC (Directorate of Defense Trade Controls).

  • Registration Requirement: Any manufacturer, broker, or exporter of defense articles on the USML must register with DDTC. Registration must be renewed annually.
  • Annual Fee: $2,750 per year for manufacturers (small business rate). Paid electronically via DDTC's D-Trade system.
  • Export Licenses: In addition to registration, individual export licenses (DSP-5, DSP-73) are required for each export of USML items. Processing time: 30–60 days. Applications cost $250.
  • Criminal Penalties: ITAR violations carry penalties up to 20 years imprisonment and $1,000,000 per violation. The State Department has prosecuted manufacturers for providing defense-related technical data to foreign nationals even within the US (deemed exports).
  • Required for Defense Contracts: DDTC registration is typically required before receiving a defense contract or purchase order from a prime contractor on defense programs. Primes like Lockheed, Raytheon, and Boeing require ITAR certification from all suppliers.
  • When You Need It: If a customer asks you to machine parts from a drawing marked "ITAR" or "USML," or if you produce components for missiles, aircraft armament, military vehicles, or classified systems — consult an ITAR compliance attorney before proceeding.

Business License & Zoning

Business License, Industrial Zoning & Local Permits

  • State Business License: Required in all states. Register entity (LLC, S-Corp, C-Corp) with the Secretary of State. Most states: $50–$300 initial filing fee.
  • EIN (Employer Identification Number): Required for any business with employees. Apply free at IRS.gov. Instant approval online.
  • Industrial Zoning: CNC shops and metal fabrication require M-1 (light manufacturing) or M-2 (heavy manufacturing) zoning. Verify before signing lease — residential and light commercial zones prohibit manufacturing. Noise ordinance compliance may require sound mitigation for operations near residential boundaries.
  • Building Permit / Certificate of Occupancy: Required for any buildout, equipment installation, or occupancy change. Fire department inspection required. CNC machines over 400 lbs require floor load rating verification (typically 250–500 lbs/sq ft for industrial).
  • County / City Business License: In addition to state licensing, most municipalities require a local business license ($25–$500/year).
  • Sales Tax Permit: Required if selling machined parts (tangible personal property) in states with sales tax. Apply with state Department of Revenue.
  • Workers Compensation Insurance: Required in virtually all states for any business with employees. Manufacturing has higher WC rates due to injury risk. Typically 2–8% of payroll for machine shop operations.

Common Questions

Manufacturing Business Licensing — FAQ

What are the OSHA standards for a CNC machine shop?
The primary OSHA standards for CNC machine shops are: 29 CFR 1910.212 (machine guarding), 29 CFR 1910.147 (lockout/tagout — written energy control procedures for every machine), 29 CFR 1910.132–138 (PPE — safety glasses, hearing protection above 85 dB, steel-toed boots), 29 CFR 1910.1000 (air contaminants — cutting fluid mist limits), and 29 CFR 1910.303 (electrical safety). Machine guarding and lockout/tagout are consistently among OSHA's top 10 most cited violations with penalties up to $15,625 per violation.
What does a lockout/tagout program require?
A compliant LOTO program under 29 CFR 1910.147 requires: (1) Written energy control program. (2) Machine-specific energy control procedures for every piece of equipment requiring maintenance. (3) Hardware: lockout padlocks, hasps, lockout stations, and tags — one lock per authorized employee. (4) Employee training for authorized (perform lockout) and affected (work in area) employees. (5) Annual periodic inspections by an authorized employee other than the one using the procedure. LOTO is consistently in OSHA's top 5 most cited violations.
How much does ISO 9001 certification cost for a small shop?
ISO 9001 certification for a small job shop (10–50 employees) typically costs $5,000–$25,000 total for the initial certification cycle: consultant fees ($3,000–$15,000 if needed), gap analysis, quality management system development, and certification body audit fees ($2,000–$8,000 for BSI, SGS, or Bureau Veritas). Annual surveillance audits run $1,500–$4,000/year. Timeline: 6–18 months. Shops doing this in-house with an experienced quality manager save significantly on consultant fees.
What is ITAR and does my shop need it?
ITAR (22 CFR Parts 120–130) controls the export and manufacture of defense articles on the US Munitions List. Your shop needs ITAR registration if you manufacture or provide services for USML items. Registration with DDTC costs $2,750/year. Criminal penalties for violations: up to 20 years imprisonment and $1,000,000 per violation. If any customer asks you to machine parts marked "ITAR" or "USML," consult an ITAR compliance attorney before proceeding.
What is the difference between AS9100 and ISO 9001 for aerospace?
ISO 9001 is the general quality management standard. AS9100 (Rev D) is the aerospace extension — it adds first article inspection (FAI per AS9102), foreign object damage (FOD) prevention, risk management, key characteristics flow-down, and configuration management. AS9100 is required by Boeing, Airbus, Lockheed Martin, and all aerospace tier-1/tier-2 suppliers. You cannot supply aerospace parts without AS9100 from an IAQG-recognized certification body. Initial AS9100 certification costs $8,000–$30,000. See our Haas CNC financing guide and Hurco financing guide for equipment to support aerospace work.
What environmental permits does a CNC shop need for coolant disposal?
Used CNC coolant is regulated under RCRA. If it exhibits hazardous characteristics (metals, pH), it must be disposed of by a licensed hazardous waste transporter with a manifest. Most shops use licensed coolant recyclers. Solvent-based degreasers (VOCs) require air quality operating permits in many states. Air quality permits are most stringent in California (CARB), Northeast states, and areas in non-attainment for ozone or particulate. Consult your state EPA/DEQ for specific thresholds and permit requirements.
Can I run a CNC machine shop in a residential or light industrial zone?
CNC machine shops require industrial zoning — typically M-1 (light manufacturing) or M-2 (heavy manufacturing). Residential zones and most commercial zones (C-1, C-2) prohibit manufacturing operations. Before signing any lease, verify zoning with the local planning/zoning department. Noise ordinances (typically 55–65 dB at property line for industrial zones adjacent to residential) may affect operational hours or require sound mitigation. Industrial zoning also affects parking requirements and loading dock access.
Do I need a CDL to deliver machined parts or operate a material delivery truck?
A CDL (Commercial Driver's License) is required for commercial vehicles with GVWR over 26,001 lbs, or combinations over 26,001 lbs with a trailer over 10,000 lbs GVWR. Most small and medium CNC shops using standard box trucks or pickup trucks within weight limits do not require CDL. If you operate a semi-truck or heavy flatbed for raw material or large part delivery, CDL Class A is required. Check your state DMV for exact weight thresholds.
