Frequently Asked Questions — Liebherr Equipment Financing

Where is Liebherr headquarters and where is equipment manufactured?

Liebherr Group is headquartered in Bulle, Switzerland (holding company) and Biberach an der Riß, Germany (operational HQ). Different product lines are manufactured at different German and Austrian plants: crawlers and excavators at Kirchdorf, Germany; mobile cranes (LTM series) at Ehingen, Germany; tower cranes at Biberach; and maritime cranes at Rostock, Germany. Liebherr USA is based in Newport News, Virginia.

How does financing work for Liebherr all-terrain cranes (LTM series)?

Liebherr Financial Services provides OEM financing for LTM cranes, but due to the specialized nature and high value ($500,000–$28M+), most LTM crane financing involves structured deals with specialty lenders. LTM cranes have excellent resale values in the global crane market — a 5-year-old LTM 1100 retains 55–65% of new value because global crane contractors actively seek Liebherr at auction. Specialty crane lenders (First National Capital, AeroCentury, Madison Capital) understand this collateral class.

What is the most financed Liebherr excavator model?

The most commonly financed Liebherr excavator models in the US are the R 922 ($212,000–$265,000) and R 924 ($225,000–$281,000) — the 22–24T class that competes with the Cat 323 and Komatsu PC240. These are the most frequently traded models on the US used market, which makes lender approval easier due to established resale data. Liebherr excavators command premium pricing in secondary markets due to German build quality and long service life.

Do US lenders finance Liebherr equipment?

Yes. Despite being European-made (Germany/Switzerland), US lenders are comfortable financing Liebherr equipment — particularly LTM cranes and R-series excavators — because both categories have deep global secondary markets with strong resale values. Independent specialty lenders (Beacon Capital, ENGS, First National Capital) often finance Liebherr at rates similar to Cat or Komatsu. The key is choosing a lender familiar with European construction equipment collateral.

What is the Liebherr LTM 11200-9.1 and why does it matter?

The Liebherr LTM 11200-9.1 ($20M–$28M) is the world's largest all-terrain mobile crane, with a 1,200-metric-ton capacity and main boom length of 108 meters (354 feet). It is used for heavy industrial lifts — refinery vessel installations, nuclear plant components, wind turbine erection at scale. Only a small number of these exist globally, and financing typically involves a consortium of lenders and/or structured project finance rather than standard equipment loans.

How does Liebherr compare to Manitowoc and Tadano for crane financing?

In the all-terrain crane market, Liebherr holds the dominant position for large-capacity cranes (100T+). Manitowoc (Manitowoc, Wisconsin — American) and Tadano (Takamatsu, Japan) are major competitors. For financing: Liebherr holds slightly higher resale values on large LTM cranes than comparable Manitowoc Grove models. Manitowoc wins on US availability and domestic manufacturer status. Tadano offers competitive pricing on mid-range AT cranes. All three are accepted by specialty crane lenders.
