Ownership & Financing Context

Alamo Group Ownership — What It Means for Morbark Financing

Morbark was acquired by Alamo Group (NYSE: ALG) in 2019 for approximately $300 million. This acquisition changed Morbark's financing profile in a meaningful way for equipment lenders:

  • Financial Stability: Alamo Group generates approximately $1.4 billion in annual revenue and is listed on the NYSE. This institutional backing assures lenders that Morbark warranty claims will be honored and parts will be available for the full life of the financed equipment.
  • Parts & Service Network: Alamo Group's distribution infrastructure expanded Morbark's parts network, reducing the risk that a financed machine becomes difficult to service — a concern lenders weigh in collateral evaluation.
  • Credit Rating: Alamo Group's investment-grade corporate profile supports Morbark Financial programs at competitive rates.
  • Comparison to Independent Brands: Compared to smaller privately-held forestry equipment manufacturers, Morbark's Alamo Group ownership provides lenders with higher confidence in long-term support — comparable to how Caterpillar's scale reassures excavator lenders.

Common Questions

Morbark Equipment Financing — FAQ

Morbark vs Vermeer — which is easier to finance?
Vermeer has a slight financing advantage over Morbark due to larger dealer network, broader general lender recognition, and a more established OEM finance program. However, both brands are American-made and equally accessible through specialty forestry equipment lenders. Morbark's acquisition by publicly-traded Alamo Group (NYSE: ALG) in 2019 adds a degree of financial stability that lenders appreciate — Alamo Group's $1.4B annual revenue ensures warranty support and parts availability. For established tree service operators and utility contractors working with specialty lenders, Morbark and Vermeer financing terms are effectively equivalent.
What is the monthly payment on a Morbark 4600XL tub grinder?
A new Morbark 4600XL tub grinder priced at $140,000-$250,000 financed over 60 months at 7% APR runs approximately $2,772-$4,950/month. At 72 months at 7%, payments drop to approximately $2,320-$4,142/month. At 84 months at 7%, payments drop further to approximately $2,010-$3,591/month. Tub grinders typically qualify for longer terms (up to 84 months) due to their high purchase price and long productive life. Used Morbark 4600XL units at $75,000-$130,000 financed over 60 months at 8% run approximately $1,520-$2,634/month. See our tree service business income guide for revenue models.
What is Alamo Group and how does it affect Morbark financing?
Alamo Group (NYSE: ALG) is a publicly-traded manufacturer of vegetation management and infrastructure maintenance equipment headquartered in Seguin, Texas. Alamo Group acquired Morbark in 2019 for approximately $300 million. With approximately $1.4 billion in annual revenue and a NYSE listing, Alamo Group provides financial backing that gives lenders additional confidence in Morbark's warranty support, parts availability, and long-term business continuity. This is a meaningful difference from smaller independent manufacturers — lenders know that Morbark warranty claims will be honored and parts will be available for years to come.
How do I finance a whole tree chipper for utility work?
Whole tree chippers for utility and line-clearing work ($200,000-$500,000) are financed through specialty construction and forestry equipment lenders. Utility contractors typically need: 2+ years in business with documented utility contract history, 650+ personal/business credit, 10-20% down payment, and proof of signed utility contracts or a master service agreement (MSA) with a utility company. Having an MSA in place before applying dramatically improves both approval odds and rate terms. Major utility contractors with established track records can often finance whole tree chippers with 10% down and 60-84 month terms.
Can I finance used Morbark equipment?
Yes. Used Morbark equipment is accepted by specialty forestry and construction equipment lenders. Alamo Group's ownership of Morbark (since 2019) gives lenders additional confidence that used equipment is backed by a company with resources to honor warranty claims and supply parts. For used Morbark equipment: typical terms are 48-60 months, 10-20% down, 7-11% APR depending on age and condition. Machines in the 3-7 year range with documented service history receive the best terms. Very high-hour or older machines may require higher down payments and shorter terms.
What warranty coverage does Morbark equipment come with?
New Morbark equipment comes with a 1-year limited warranty covering defects in materials and workmanship. Extended warranty and service agreements are available through Morbark dealers. Under Alamo Group ownership, Morbark has maintained its warranty support infrastructure and expanded parts availability through Alamo Group's distribution network. For financing purposes, lenders consider warranty coverage when evaluating collateral risk — a machine under warranty has lower risk of becoming a non-performing asset. If you have questions about less familiar brands and how warranty affects financing, see our guide on financing lesser-known equipment brands.
Can a startup tree service finance Morbark equipment?
Yes, with the right approach. Startup tree service businesses (under 2 years) financing Morbark equipment typically need: 680+ personal credit, 20-30% down payment, documentation of arborist or tree service industry experience (ISA certification, prior employment), and ideally a signed contract from a first customer. Starting with a Morbark 2060 or 2260 drum chipper ($35,000-$75,000) is much more accessible than a $200,000+ tub grinder on day one. Build 12-24 months of revenue and payment history before applying for larger machines. See our how to start a tree service business guide and tree service licensing requirements guide.
What is the difference between a tub grinder and horizontal grinder for biomass?
For biomass operations, horizontal grinders (Morbark 2400XL-7600) are generally preferred over tub grinders for large-volume production grinding of slash, land clearing debris, and urban wood waste. Horizontal grinders offer higher throughput per hour, easier infeed for mixed material, and better contamination control. Tub grinders (Morbark 3036-5036) excel at processing whole logs, stumps, and large brush piles — they are more common in land clearing, stump disposal, and smaller biomass operations. Horizontal grinders in the $280,000-$600,000 range are a significant capital investment but are the industry standard for utility-scale biomass fuel production.
