What manufacturing equipment financing covers

Manufacturing equipment financing is a secured loan or lease used to buy production machinery. The equipment is collateral; payments align with useful life. Job shops, OEM suppliers, and contract manufacturers use it to preserve cash for tooling and labor.

1. Choose your machine class and obtain a dealer quote. 2. Submit financials and the invoice. 3. The lender approves against collateral value and your credit. 4. The lender funds the vendor; you take delivery and begin monthly payments.

Brands with deep used markets—Haas 🇺🇸, Mazak 🇯🇵, Trumpf 🇩🇪—typically receive faster approvals than obscure or imported specialty lines.

Licensing & compliance

Topic sentence: most shops need a business license, OSHA machine guarding under 29 CFR 1910.212, lockout/tagout 1910.147, and electrical permits for 3-phase drops. Aerospace suppliers often pursue ISO 9001 or AS9100; defense work may require ITAR registration. Environmental rules may cover coolant disposal and laser exhaust.

Income potential

Topic sentence: revenue scales with spindle hours and billing rates. A small job shop with 1–2 CNC machines may produce roughly $150K–$400K revenue; growing shops with 3–6 machines often reach $500K–$1.5M; highly automated shops can exceed $2M–$8M. Hourly rates of $75–$150 for standard 3-axis work and $150–$250 for 5-axis are common benchmarks.

Common Questions

Manufacturing Equipment Financing — Frequently Asked Questions

What is the monthly payment for CNC financing?
Monthly payments depend on amount financed, rate, and term. A $120,000 CNC at 8% over 60 months is roughly $2,400–$2,500 per month before fees; shorter terms raise the payment but reduce total interest. Your lender will provide an amortization schedule after approval.
Can I finance used CNC equipment?
Yes. Lenders cap age and hours—often 10–15 years or newer—and require inspection or auction reports. Strong brands like Haas and Mazak finance more easily than one-off imports. Expect slightly higher rates than new and shorter maximum terms.
What credit score is needed for manufacturing equipment?
Many banks prefer 650+; equipment finance companies may approve at 600+ with strong collateral. Alternative lenders sometimes work from 500+ with larger down payments when the machine has verified resale value.
Should I lease or buy a CNC machine?
Loans build equity and suit long-lived machine tools. Operating leases help when you want lower payments and plan to refresh technology. Tax treatment differs—consult a CPA about Section 179 vs lease expense rules for your situation.
How long does manufacturing equipment approval take?
Straightforward applications with clean credit often close in 24–72 hours. Larger amounts, used machines, or complex financials may take a week or more for underwriting and title/lien perfection.
Can a startup machine shop get financing?
Startups usually need 680+ personal credit, 20–30% down, and a credible business plan. Financing recognizable brands with strong auction values improves odds compared to exotic or single-source machines.
Does Section 179 apply to financed CNC purchases?
Section 179 can allow immediate expensing of qualifying equipment placed in service the same tax year, subject to annual limits and phase-outs. Financing does not disqualify the deduction in many cases—verify with your tax advisor.
How do lenders value manufacturing equipment as collateral?
Underwriters use auction comps, dealer quotes, and guidebooks. Popular models with liquid resale receive higher advance rates; customized or obsolete configurations receive haircuts. Serial numbers, photos, and maintenance records help.
